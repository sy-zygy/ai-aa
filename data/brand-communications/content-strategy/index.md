# Content Strategy

**Owner:** Communications Director

The editorial plan for AI-AA's public content. Every piece of content should advance at least one stage of the growth flywheel: stories attract attention, attention validates the cause, validation attracts sponsors.

## Content Pillars

All content maps to one of four messaging pillars (from [[Brand & Voice Guide]]):

1. **This is a civil-rights issue, not a tech issue** — AI comprehension tools are modern auxiliary aids
2. **People are already being harmed** — Real harm from blocked AI comprehension access
3. **This is good for everyone** — Universal design benefits beyond disability
4. **We are narrowly focused and legally grounded** — Existing law, specific ask, achievable goal

## Blog / Publication Platform Recommendation

**Recommendation: LinkedIn Articles (immediate) + dedicated website blog section (medium-term)**

### Phase 1 — Launch (Now): LinkedIn Articles
- **Zero setup cost.** Once the LinkedIn company page exists, we can publish long-form articles immediately.
- **Built-in distribution.** LinkedIn articles surface to followers and get indexed by search engines. Our primary sponsor audience (CSR/policy leads at tech companies) lives on LinkedIn.
- **Credibility signal.** A LinkedIn presence with substantive articles reads as professional and established — exactly the positioning we need.
- **The feature article** ("When AI Is the Only Interpreter in the Room") should debut as a LinkedIn article in Week 3.

### Phase 2 — Website Blog (Q2 2026)
- Add a `/blog` or `/articles` section to the AI-AA website. This gives us a permanent, owned home for all long-form content.
- Cross-post LinkedIn articles to the website blog for SEO and archival.
- The website blog becomes the canonical URL we link from social media, press materials, and coalition communications.

### Why not Medium/Substack?
- **Medium:** Paywalls alienate readers. We preach accessibility — gating content behind a subscription contradicts the brand. Also, we don't own the platform.
- **Substack:** Better for personal voices than organizational ones. The newsletter format doesn't match our content types (we need articles, explainers, spotlights — not a personal newsletter voice). Also carries political associations that could complicate our positioning.
- **Both:** We lose SEO juice to their domains instead of building our own. For a rights-based organization, owning our content platform matters.

### Newsletter (Future)
- Once we have a subscriber base (from website signups, social media CTAs), add a bi-weekly email newsletter.
- Can use any email tool (Buttondown, Mailchimp, Resend) — the website blog is the content source, the newsletter is the distribution channel.

**Decision needed from CEO:** Approve LinkedIn Articles as Phase 1 blog platform, website blog section as Phase 2.

## Content Quality Checklist

Before publishing any content:
- [ ] Does it align with one of the four messaging pillars?
- [ ] Does it lead with a person, not a policy abstraction?
- [ ] Is it written in plain language (grade 8 reading level for public content)?
- [ ] Are all claims evidence-backed and sourced?
- [ ] Does it follow the [[Brand & Voice Guide]] voice principles?
- [ ] Has it been reviewed for legal accuracy on any claims about the law? (coordinate with Policy)
- [ ] Does it advance at least one stage of the growth flywheel?
