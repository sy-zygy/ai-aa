## Educational / Explainer Posts

### E1 — LinkedIn: "What is an auxiliary aid?"

> **What is an "auxiliary aid" — and why does it matter for AI?**
>
> Under the Americans with Disabilities Act, covered entities must provide "auxiliary aids and services" to ensure effective communication with people who have disabilities.
>
> This is a broad, technology-neutral category. It was designed to evolve.
>
> In 1990, auxiliary aids meant TTY devices and large-print documents.
> In 2000, it meant screen readers and captioning.
> In 2010, it meant accessible websites and video relay services.
>
> In 2026, it should include AI tools that help people comprehend complex written information.
>
> The legal principle hasn't changed: people with disabilities have the right to understand information that affects their lives. The tools have changed. The law is built to accommodate that.
>
> This is the argument AI Access Alliance is building — not for new legislation, but for applying existing law to the tools people already depend on.
