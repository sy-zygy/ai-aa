# Coalition Communications

**Owner:** Communications Director

Materials and coordination for AI-AA's coalition-building efforts. The coalition statement is a Year One milestone — a cross-sector sign-on letter demonstrating that disability-rights organizations, legal experts, and accessibility practitioners support AI comprehension access as a civil-rights issue.

## Coalition Statement

**Target:** Launch by Q3 2026

The statement should:
- Affirm the principle that AI-mediated comprehension is a legitimate auxiliary aid
- Be signable by disability-rights organizations, legal-aid groups, accessibility practitioners, and academic institutions
- Avoid language that could be perceived as serving AI industry commercial interests
- Include specific examples and legal references
- Be written in plain language (practice what we preach)

**Status:** Not drafted

## Sensitive Partnerships

### ASAN (Autistic Self Advocacy Network)
ASAN has published opposition to generative AI in plain-language contexts. Approach with respect:
- Acknowledge their reliability and consent concerns directly
- Emphasize that AI-AA supports user choice, not mandated AI replacement of human plain-language writers
- Position AI comprehension tools as supplements, not replacements
- Invite dialogue, not sign-on — they may not sign, but respectful engagement prevents active opposition

### Organizations with Corporate Ties
Any coalition partner that also receives funding from AI companies should be documented transparently. We can't prevent overlap, but we can be transparent about it.
