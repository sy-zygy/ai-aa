# Brand & Communications

**Owner:** Communications Director | **Status:** Active | **Updated:** 2026-04-05

The Communications department is AI-AA's public voice. Every word that reaches the outside world — social media, website, press, coalition statements, sponsor announcements — passes through this department. We drive the growth flywheel: human stories attract public attention, attention validates the cause, validation attracts sponsors, sponsors fund more work, work produces more stories.

## Department Scope

This department leads the **Coalition & Public Education workstream** — one of AI-AA's four charter workstreams.

### What's In Scope

- **Brand management** — Maintaining consistent voice, visual identity, and messaging across all channels
- **Social media** — Content creation, posting, community engagement on LinkedIn and X/Twitter
- **Content strategy** — Blog posts, newsletters, explainer content, educational materials
- **Media relations** — Journalist relationships, press kits, spokesperson preparation, reactive media
- **Coalition communications** — Joint statements, partner toolkits, sign-on letters, coalition announcements
- **Website content** — Homepage copy, about pages, resource pages (not technical development)
- **Sponsor-visible communications** — Coordinated announcements of partnerships and milestones

### What's Out of Scope

- Legal analysis of claims in public content (that's Policy — we coordinate for review)
- Original research or evidence gathering (that's Research — we tell their stories)
- Sponsor relationship management (that's Development — we support with announcements)
- Website technical development (separate concern)

## Mission Contribution

Communications is the amplifier that converts internal work into external impact:

| Department | What They Need From Us |
|------------|----------------------|
| **Development** | Public traction validates the cause for sponsors. Sponsor announcements generate visibility for their investment. We make the sponsor pitch tangible. |
| **Policy** | Accessible summaries of legal arguments reach policymakers who wouldn't read a 25-page white paper. Public pressure supports guidance petition requests. |
| **Research** | Public storytelling creates inbound interest from people who want to share their own experiences. Our reach expands the evidence base. |
| **Operations** | Coalition communications attract advisory council candidates. Public credibility supports board recruitment. |

## Brand Foundation

**Tagline:** *Everyone deserves to understand.*

**Voice principles:**
1. **Rights-first** — AI access is a civil-rights issue, not a tech feature
2. **Human-centered** — Lead with people, not products
3. **Plain language** — Practice what we preach
4. **Credible, not combative** — Build coalitions, not enemies
5. **Specific, not vague** — Name the settings, the laws, the people

See [[Brand & Voice Guide]] for the complete brand identity, audience personas, messaging pillars, visual identity direction, and tone-by-channel guidelines.

## Content Strategy

### Social Media Channels

| Channel | Audience | Cadence | Content Focus |
|---------|----------|---------|---------------|
| **LinkedIn** | Sponsors, policymakers, professionals | 3-5 posts/week | Policy updates, thought leadership, sponsor announcements, coalition milestones |
| **X/Twitter** | Public, advocates, journalists | 5-7 posts/week | Human stories, advocacy threads, news reactions, coalition building |

### Content Types (Priority Order)

1. **Human stories** — Real people, real impact, real stakes. The most important content type. Sourced from Research Director.
2. **Policy explainers** — Make the legal argument accessible. "Did you know the ADA already covers this?"
3. **Sector spotlights** — Deep dives into healthcare, education, government, legal, employment.
4. **Expert voices** — Op-eds, interviews, advisory council perspectives.
5. **Progress updates** — Milestone announcements, coalition growth, sponsor reveals.
6. **Educational content** — "What is an auxiliary aid?" "How does effective communication work?"

### Content Calendar Cadence

| Frequency | Content | Owner |
|-----------|---------|-------|
| Daily | Social media posts (LinkedIn, X) | Communications Director |
| Weekly | 1 blog post or longform LinkedIn article | Communications Director |
| Bi-weekly | Newsletter (when subscriber base exists) | Communications Director |
| Monthly | Sector spotlight deep dive | Communications Director + Research Director |
| As needed | Press releases, coalition statements, reactive media | Communications Director |

## Operating Model

### How This Department Works

- **Stories first, always.** A single well-told human story outperforms a hundred policy posts. Lead with people.
- **Consistent cadence.** Post regularly. A dormant social presence is worse than no presence.
- **Respond to news cycles.** When AI access or disability rights are in the news, react within hours with AI-AA's perspective.
- **Amplify, don't just broadcast.** Engage with community voices. Quote disability advocates. Share partner content. Build relationships, not just an audience.
- **Brand consistency.** Every piece of content should feel like it came from the same organization.

### Two Threats to Monitor

1. **"Industry front" criticism** — Counter by centering disability voices, leading with human stories, emphasizing governance firewall.
2. **Disability community skepticism** — Engage respectfully with ASAN and other skeptics. Acknowledge reliability concerns. Emphasize user choice, not mandated AI.

### Cross-Department Coordination

- **With Research:** Request specific user stories for planned content. Get statistics and evidence for claims. All public-facing claims reviewed for accuracy.
- **With Development:** Coordinate sponsor announcements. Align messaging for sponsor-visible materials. Pitch materials share brand voice.
- **With Policy:** Get legal framing for public messaging. Have Policy review content for accuracy on legal claims.
- **With Operations:** Coalition communications support advisory council recruitment. Board announcements coordinate with Operations.
