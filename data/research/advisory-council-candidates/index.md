## Disability Rights Law Scholars (3 candidates)

### 1. Peter Blanck — Syracuse University / Burton Blatt Institute
Author of *eQuality: The Struggle for Web Accessibility by Persons with Cognitive Disabilities* (Cambridge, 2014) — literally the book on our core issue. Holds the highest faculty rank at Syracuse (University Professor). His BBI has global reach and his work specifically argues that people with cognitive disabilities have legal rights to equal web access using emerging technologies.

### 2. Claudia Center — Disability Rights Education and Defense Fund (DREDF)
Legal Director of DREDF with U.S. Supreme Court argument experience on reasonable accommodations (US Airways v. Barnett). Her litigation/policy/legislative expertise means she can pressure-test our legal arguments from a practitioner's perspective. DREDF is one of our priority source organizations.

### 3. Lainey Feingold — Independent Practice
Negotiated the first web accessibility agreement in the U.S. (2000) and pioneered Structured Negotiation as an alternative to litigation. Her collaborative, solutions-oriented approach aligns perfectly with AI-AA's guidance-first strategy. 30+ years in digital accessibility law.

## Cognitive/Learning Disability Researchers (2 candidates)

### 4. Jonathan Lazar — University of Maryland / Trace R&D Center
Directs the TRACE Center (created the first web accessibility guidelines, 1995). Rare combination: leading accessibility researcher who also holds an LLM in disability rights law. His dual expertise means he can evaluate AI-AA's arguments from both technical feasibility and legal validity angles.

### 5. Meryl Alper — Northeastern University
Three MIT Press books on communication technology and disability, including *Giving Voice* and *Kids Across the Spectrums*. Studies how people with disabilities actually use communication technologies — the same dynamic AI-AA is mapping for AI comprehension tools. Sesame Workshop/PBS background gives education-sector credibility.

Her co-design methodology was adopted by Microsoft. UN and White House appearances demonstrate policy influence.

## Health Literacy / Plain Language (2 candidates)

### 7. Cynthia Baur — University of Maryland / Horowitz Center for Health Literacy
17+ years at CDC as Plain Language and Health Literacy Lead. Co-created the CDC Clear Communication Index. Led the National Action Plan to Improve Health Literacy. The foremost U.S. expert on the exact comprehension gap AI tools fill. Direct relationships with HHS (one of our target agencies).

### 8. Rylin Rodgers — Microsoft Accessibility (cross-listed: lived experience)
Disability Policy Director at Microsoft. Published "AI Can Create Plain Language and Easy Read Versions of Every Written Material on Earth" (University of Minnesota). Dyslexic, has IPF, mother of two young adults with physical disabilities. Her CDT advisory committee membership connects to board candidate Aboulafia. **COI note:** Microsoft employee; must be disclosed but strengthens rather than weakens credibility under advisory (non-governance) terms.
