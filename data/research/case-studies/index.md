# Sector Case Studies

**Owner:** Research Director | **Target:** 5 case studies by Q3 2026

Each case study documents a specific institutional context — a hospital, university, government agency, court system, or employer — where AI comprehension support is needed, blocked, or already working. These are deeper than user stories: they examine the institution, its policies, the regulatory framework, and the impact on people who need comprehension support.

## Case Study Template

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Institution type** | Hospital, university, agency, court, employer |
| **Specific institution** | Named if public, anonymized if needed |
| **Regulatory framework** | Which laws apply (ADA Title II/III, Section 504, Section 1557, etc.) |
| **Current AI policy** | Does the institution block, allow, or have no policy on AI comprehension tools? |
| **Affected population** | Who is harmed by the current policy (or lack thereof)? |
| **Key documents** | What documents create comprehension barriers? |
| **Evidence of harm** | Documented cases of harm from blocked comprehension access |
| **Model policy potential** | What would a good policy look like for this institution? |
| **Sources** | Published policies, news reports, legal filings, advocacy reports |

## Planned Case Studies

### 1. Healthcare — Patient Portal AI Blocking
*Status: Not started*
Focus: Major hospital systems that block AI tools on patient portals. Impact on patients trying to understand discharge instructions, medication guides, and consent forms.

### 2. Education — University AI Policies and 504 Students
*Status: Not started*
Focus: Universities with blanket AI bans that don't exempt disability accommodations. Impact on students with IEPs/504 plans who rely on AI for reading comprehension.

### 3. Government — Benefits Application Complexity
*Status: Not started*
Focus: SSA, Medicaid, or VA benefits systems where application complexity creates comprehension barriers. How AI could help — and what blocks it.

### 4. Legal — Self-Represented Litigants
*Status: Not started*
Focus: Court systems where self-represented litigants face complex legal documents. How AI comprehension tools could serve as de facto legal-aid supplements.

### 5. Employment — Workplace AI Policies
*Status: Not started*
Focus: Employers whose AI-blocking policies prevent employees from using comprehension aids for benefits enrollment, policy documents, or training materials.

## Research Approach

1. Identify specific institutions through news reports, advocacy organizations, and legal filings
2. Document their current AI policies (or lack thereof)
3. Interview affected individuals or advocates (where possible)
4. Analyze the applicable regulatory framework
5. Draft a model policy recommendation for the sector
6. Have Policy Director review for legal accuracy
