## Candidate Criteria

**Must have:**
- Public commitment to disability rights (not just general DEI)
- Relevant expertise in law, policy, technology, or disability advocacy
- No disqualifying conflicts of interest (no current sponsor employees)
- Willingness to serve on a startup non-profit board (time commitment, no compensation initially)

**Should have:**
- Geographic and demographic diversity
- Mix of established names (credibility) and emerging voices (energy)
- Network connections to disability-rights organizations, policymakers, or funders
- Experience on non-profit boards or advisory bodies

## Process

1. **Research** — Identify candidates from source organizations
2. **Profile** — Create candidate brief (background, expertise, alignment, concerns)
3. **Vet** — Check for conflicts of interest, public positions, controversies
4. **Approach** — Warm introduction where possible, then mission brief + commitment overview
5. **Interview** — Discuss mission alignment, time commitment, governance expectations
6. **Appoint** — Board vote (once initial board seated), onboarding

Each candidate gets a sub-page in this section with full profile and tracking.
