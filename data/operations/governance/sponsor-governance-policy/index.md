# Sponsor Governance Policy

**Status:** DRAFT v0.1 | **Last Updated:** 2026-04-05 | **Owner:** Operations Director

*This policy establishes the structural separation between corporate sponsorship and organizational governance at AI Access Alliance. It is incorporated into the Bylaws by reference (Article VII) and may only be amended by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the full Board.*

---

## 1. Purpose

AI Access Alliance accepts corporate sponsorships to fund its mission of advancing equitable access to AI for people with disabilities and neurodivergent individuals. This policy ensures that financial support does not translate into influence over the Organization's mission, governance, legal positions, policy recommendations, research conclusions, or enforcement decisions.

The integrity of this separation is existential to the Organization's credibility. If sponsors can influence what AI-AA says, publishes, or advocates, the Organization becomes an industry front group — regardless of the quality of its work.

---

## 2. Definitions

- **Sponsor**: Any corporation, foundation, organization, or individual that provides financial support to AI-AA under a sponsorship agreement, including Founding Sponsors, Corporate Partners, and annual or multi-year contributors.
- **Sponsor Representative**: Any individual who is a current employee, officer, director, or paid consultant of a Sponsor, or any individual designated by a Sponsor to represent its interests.
- **Sponsor-Affiliated Individual**: Any individual who has been employed by, consulted for, or held a board seat at a Sponsor within the preceding twenty-four (24) months.
- **Policy Position**: Any legal analysis, regulatory recommendation, guidance petition, amicus brief, public comment, research finding, model policy, or public statement issued by or on behalf of AI-AA.

---

## 3. Core Governance Restrictions

### 3.1 — No Board Seats for Sponsors
No Sponsor Representative or Sponsor-Affiliated Individual may hold a voting seat on the Board of Directors. This restriction applies regardless of the size of the sponsorship or any other consideration.

**Rationale:** Board seats grant fiduciary authority and voting power over organizational direction. Even a single sponsor-affiliated board member creates a structural conflict — and a visible one that critics will exploit.

### 3.2 — No Veto Power
No Sponsor shall have veto power, approval authority, or sign-off rights over any:
- Policy Position
- Legal filing or amicus brief
- Research publication or report
- Enforcement target or regulatory complaint
- Grant application or funding request
- Hiring or personnel decision
- Partnership or coalition decision

### 3.3 — No Pre-Publication Review
Sponsors shall not receive advance copies, previews, or embargoed access to Policy Positions, research publications, or legal filings prior to public release. Sponsors learn about AI-AA's positions when the public does.

**Exception:** AI-AA may notify a Sponsor that a forthcoming publication will reference or analyze that Sponsor's products or practices, and may offer a factual accuracy check limited to verifiable claims of fact (e.g., product availability, feature specifications). This notification:
- Must occur no more than 48 hours before publication
- Must be limited to factual claims, not legal analysis or policy conclusions
- Must not result in changes to legal analysis, policy recommendations, or editorial framing
- Must be documented in writing

### 3.4 — No Influence Over Enforcement
Sponsors shall not influence, directly or indirectly, any decision regarding:
- Which companies, products, or practices are subjects of policy analysis or criticism
- Whether to file regulatory complaints, guidance petitions, or amicus briefs
- The content of public comments submitted to regulatory agencies
- Coalition positions or joint advocacy efforts

### 3.5 — No Conditional Funding
Sponsorship agreements shall not include any provision that conditions funding on:
- AI-AA taking or refraining from taking a specific Policy Position
- AI-AA including or excluding a specific company, product, or practice from analysis
- AI-AA hiring or refraining from hiring specific individuals
- AI-AA partnering or refraining from partnering with specific organizations

Any attempt by a Sponsor to impose such conditions shall be reported to the Board and documented.

---

## 4. Sponsor Participation Channels

### 4.1 — Advisory Council Seats
Sponsors may nominate representatives for non-voting Advisory Council seats (2-3 seats reserved for sponsor representatives). Sponsor Advisory Council members:
- Are clearly designated as sponsor-affiliated in all communications
- Must recuse themselves from discussions where their employer's direct interests are at stake
- May not participate in the development of Policy Positions
- Provide input on industry trends, technical capabilities, and implementation considerations
- Have no governance authority

### 4.2 — Annual Sponsor Convening
AI-AA shall host an annual convening for Sponsors to:
- Hear presentations on AI-AA's programmatic work and plans
- Provide feedback on industry trends and accessibility challenges
- Discuss implementation approaches (not policy conclusions)
- Network with disability-rights organizations and other stakeholders

This convening is informational and consultative. No governance decisions are made at or influenced by this event.

### 4.3 — Sponsor Recognition
Sponsors receive public recognition appropriate to their tier, as defined in sponsorship agreements. Recognition is limited to:
- Logo placement on website, reports, and event materials
- Acknowledgment in annual reports
- Access to branded "AI-AA Sponsor" designation
- Invitations to public events and convenings

Recognition does not imply endorsement of any Sponsor's products, practices, or accessibility claims.

---

## 5. Sponsorship Agreement Requirements

All sponsorship agreements must include the following provisions, in substantially this form:

> **Governance Independence.** Sponsor acknowledges and agrees that this sponsorship does not confer any voting rights, veto authority, or governance role within AI Access Alliance. AI-AA retains sole and exclusive authority over its policy positions, legal filings, research publications, enforcement decisions, hiring, partnerships, and all other organizational decisions. Sponsor shall not condition any portion of this sponsorship on AI-AA taking or refraining from taking any specific position or action.

> **No Pre-Publication Review.** Sponsor acknowledges that it will not receive advance access to AI-AA's policy positions, research publications, or legal filings prior to public release, except for limited factual accuracy checks as described in the Sponsor Governance Policy.

> **Public Disclosure.** AI-AA will publicly disclose the names and sponsorship tiers of all Sponsors. Specific financial amounts may be disclosed in aggregate or individually, at AI-AA's discretion.

The Sponsorships Director shall ensure these provisions are included in all sponsorship agreements. The Operations Director shall review each agreement for governance compliance before execution.

---

## 6. Disclosure and Transparency

### 6.1 — Public Sponsor List
AI-AA shall maintain a publicly accessible list of all current Sponsors and their tier levels on its website.

### 6.2 — Annual Financial Disclosure
AI-AA shall disclose aggregate sponsorship revenue in its annual report and IRS Form 990. Individual sponsor amounts may be disclosed at AI-AA's discretion.

### 6.3 — Conflict Disclosure in Publications
Any AI-AA publication that analyzes, references, or is relevant to a specific Sponsor's products or practices shall include a disclosure noting the sponsorship relationship.

### 6.4 — Board Disclosure
The Board shall receive a report at each regular meeting listing all active sponsorship agreements and any sponsor-related governance concerns.

---

## 7. Violation and Enforcement

### 7.1 — Reporting
Any Director, officer, employee, or Advisory Council member who becomes aware of a potential violation of this policy shall report it to the Board Chair or the Governance Committee.

### 7.2 — Investigation
The Governance Committee shall investigate reported violations and present findings to the Board.

### 7.3 — Consequences
If the Board determines that a Sponsor has violated this policy (e.g., by attempting to condition funding on a policy outcome), the Board may:
1. Issue a formal warning to the Sponsor
2. Suspend the sponsorship relationship
3. Terminate the sponsorship agreement
4. Publicly disclose the violation, if the Board determines disclosure is necessary to protect AI-AA's credibility

### 7.4 — Internal Violations
If a Director, officer, or employee is found to have facilitated sponsor influence in violation of this policy, they shall be subject to removal, termination, or other appropriate action.

---

## 8. Amendment

This policy may only be amended by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the full Board of Directors (not merely those present at a meeting). Proposed amendments must be distributed to all Directors at least fourteen (14) days before the vote.
