# Conflict of Interest Policy

**Status:** DRAFT v0.1 | **Last Updated:** 2026-04-06 | **Owner:** Operations Director

*This policy governs conflicts of interest for Directors, officers, Advisory Council members, and key employees of AI Access Alliance. It is incorporated into the Bylaws by reference (Article IX) and satisfies IRS requirements for 501(c)(3) organizations.*

---

## 1. Purpose

AI Access Alliance's credibility depends on its independence — from corporate sponsors, from political interests, and from any individual's personal financial interests. This policy establishes procedures for identifying, disclosing, and managing conflicts of interest so that organizational decisions serve the mission, not private benefit.

This policy is required by the IRS for 501(c)(3) tax-exempt organizations and is modeled on IRS Form 1023 guidance.

---

## 2. Definitions

- **Interested Person**: Any Director, officer, Advisory Council member, or key employee who has a direct or indirect Financial Interest, as defined below.
- **Financial Interest**: A person has a financial interest if they have, directly or indirectly, through business, investment, or family:
  1. An ownership or investment interest in any entity with which AI-AA has a transaction or arrangement
  2. A compensation arrangement with AI-AA or with any entity with which AI-AA has a transaction or arrangement
  3. A potential ownership or investment interest in, or compensation arrangement with, any entity with which AI-AA is negotiating a transaction or arrangement
- **Compensation**: Direct or indirect remuneration, including gifts, favors, or anything of value exceeding $250 in aggregate per calendar year.
- **Sponsor-Related Conflict**: A conflict that arises because a person's interests are aligned with those of a corporate sponsor, including but not limited to: current or recent employment by a sponsor, consulting arrangements with a sponsor, investment in a sponsor, or family members employed by a sponsor.
- **Policy-Related Conflict**: A conflict that arises because an AI-AA policy position, legal filing, or research publication could materially benefit or harm an entity in which an Interested Person has a Financial Interest.

---

## 3. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All members of the Board of Directors
- All officers of the Organization
- All members of the Advisory Council (including sponsor representatives)
- All employees with decision-making authority over organizational policy, finances, or programs ("key employees")
- All committee members, whether or not they are Directors

---

## 4. Duty to Disclose

### 4.1 — General Disclosure
Each Covered Person shall disclose to the Board or appropriate committee any actual or potential conflict of interest as soon as they become aware of it, and in any event before any discussion or vote on the relevant matter.

### 4.2 — Sponsor-Related Disclosure
Given AI-AA's structural commitment to independence from sponsor influence (see Sponsor Governance Policy), Covered Persons must disclose:
- Any current employment, consulting, or compensation arrangement with any AI-AA sponsor
- Any such arrangement that ended within the preceding twenty-four (24) months
- Any family member (spouse, parent, child, sibling) currently employed by or consulting for an AI-AA sponsor
- Any investment interest exceeding $5,000 in any AI-AA sponsor
- Any negotiation or discussion regarding future employment or compensation with an AI-AA sponsor

### 4.3 — Policy-Related Disclosure
When AI-AA is developing a policy position, legal filing, or research publication that could materially affect a specific company, product, or industry sector, Covered Persons must disclose any Financial Interest in the affected entity or sector.

### 4.4 — Ongoing Duty
The duty to disclose is ongoing. New conflicts must be disclosed as they arise, not only at annual certification.

---

## 5. Procedures for Addressing Conflicts

### 5.1 — Disclosure to the Board
The Interested Person shall disclose the conflict of interest and all material facts to the Board or the relevant committee.

### 5.2 — Recusal
After disclosure, the Interested Person shall leave the meeting during the discussion and vote on the matter giving rise to the conflict. The Interested Person:
- May not participate in deliberation on the matter
- May not vote on the matter
- May not attempt to influence the deliberation or vote through informal channels
- Shall be recorded as recused in the meeting minutes

### 5.3 — Determination by Disinterested Directors
After the Interested Person has left the meeting, the remaining Board or committee members shall:
1. Determine whether a conflict of interest exists
2. If a conflict exists, determine whether the Organization can obtain a more advantageous transaction or arrangement with reasonable efforts from a person or entity that would not give rise to a conflict
3. If a more advantageous arrangement is not reasonably possible, determine by a majority vote of disinterested Directors whether the transaction is in the Organization's best interest, is fair and reasonable, and whether to proceed

### 5.4 — Documentation
The minutes of any meeting at which a conflict is disclosed and addressed shall record:
- The nature of the conflict
- The Interested Person's disclosure
- The recusal
- The deliberation and vote of disinterested Directors
- The decision reached

## 6. Sponsor-Specific Provisions

These provisions address the unique conflict-of-interest risks created by AI-AA's corporate sponsorship model.

### 6.1 — No Sponsor-Affiliated Board Members
Consistent with the Bylaws (Section 3.3) and Sponsor Governance Policy (Section 3.1), no person who is a current employee, officer, director, or paid consultant of an AI-AA sponsor may serve on the Board. This restriction also applies to any person who held such a position within the preceding twenty-four (24) months.

### 6.2 — Sponsor Advisory Council Members
Sponsor representatives on the Advisory Council are inherently conflicted with respect to matters affecting their employer. They must:
- Recuse from any discussion of policy positions, legal filings, or research that specifically analyzes or references their employer's products or practices
- Not participate in the development of policy positions (per Sponsor Governance Policy Section 4.1)
- Disclose any change in their employment status to the Board within thirty (30) days

### 6.3 — Post-Sponsorship Employment
If any Director, officer, or key employee enters into employment negotiations with a current or prospective AI-AA sponsor, they must:
1. Disclose the negotiations to the Board Chair immediately
2. Recuse from all matters involving that sponsor
3. If they accept employment with the sponsor, resign from their Board seat or organizational role

### 6.4 — Cooling-Off Period
A person who leaves employment with an AI-AA sponsor must wait twenty-four (24) months before being eligible for a Board seat. Advisory Council membership may be considered after twelve (12) months, subject to Board approval.

## 7. Annual Disclosure and Certification

### 7.1 — Annual Statement
Each Covered Person shall annually sign a statement that:
1. Affirms they have received, read, and understand this Conflict of Interest Policy
2. Agrees to comply with the policy
3. Discloses all Financial Interests, sponsor relationships, and other interests that could give rise to a conflict

### 7.2 — Timing
Annual certifications shall be completed:
- Upon initial appointment or hiring
- Annually thereafter, no later than January 31 of each calendar year
- Upon any material change in circumstances during the year

### 7.3 — Review
The Governance Committee shall review all annual certifications and report any concerns to the Board.

---

## 8. Violations

### 8.1 — Investigation
If the Board has reasonable cause to believe a Covered Person has failed to disclose an actual or potential conflict of interest, it shall inform the person and allow them to explain the alleged failure.

### 8.2 — Corrective Action
If the Board determines that a failure to disclose occurred, it shall take appropriate corrective action, which may include:
- A formal warning
- Removal from the relevant committee or advisory body
- Removal from the Board (by two-thirds vote per Bylaws Section 3.6)
- Termination of employment
- Voiding or renegotiating the conflicted transaction

### 8.3 — Documentation
All investigations and corrective actions shall be documented in Board minutes.

---

## 9. Records

The Secretary shall maintain:
- A copy of each annual disclosure and certification
- A record of all conflicts disclosed and actions taken
- Minutes documenting conflict-related deliberations and votes

Records shall be retained for at least seven (7) years.

---

## 10. Amendment

This policy may be amended by a majority vote of the Board. Proposed amendments must be distributed to all Directors at least fourteen (14) days before the vote.
