### Section 4 — Governance Independence (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

**4.1.** Sponsor acknowledges and agrees that this sponsorship does not confer any voting rights, veto authority, board seats, or governance role within AI Access Alliance. AI-AA retains sole and exclusive authority over its:

- Policy positions and legal analysis
- Legal filings, amicus briefs, and regulatory comments
- Research publications and reports
- Enforcement targets and regulatory complaints
- Hiring and personnel decisions
- Partnerships and coalition decisions
- All other organizational decisions

**4.2.** Sponsor shall not condition any portion of this sponsorship on AI-AA taking or refraining from taking any specific position or action.

**4.3.** Sponsor acknowledges that AI-AA may publish policy positions, legal analysis, or research that is critical of Sponsor's products, practices, or industry sector. Such publications are within AI-AA's mission and shall not constitute a breach of this Agreement.

**4.4.** Any attempt by Sponsor to influence AI-AA's positions, enforcement decisions, or organizational direction — whether through express conditions, implied expectations, informal communications, or threats to withdraw funding — shall be reported to the AI-AA Board of Directors and may result in suspension or termination of this Agreement.

### Section 5 — No Pre-Publication Review

**5.1.** Sponsor shall not receive advance copies, previews, or embargoed access to AI-AA's policy positions, research publications, legal filings, or regulatory comments prior to public release. Sponsor learns about AI-AA's positions when the public does.

**5.2. Limited Exception — Factual Accuracy Check.** If a forthcoming AI-AA publication specifically references or analyzes Sponsor's products or practices, AI-AA may — at its sole discretion — notify Sponsor and offer a factual accuracy review, subject to the following constraints:

- Notification occurs no more than forty-eight (48) hours before publication
- Review is limited to verifiable claims of fact (e.g., product availability, feature specifications, pricing)
- Review does not extend to legal analysis, policy conclusions, editorial framing, or recommendations
- AI-AA retains sole discretion over whether to accept, reject, or modify any factual correction
- All factual accuracy review communications shall be documented in writing

### Section 6 — Sponsor Advisory Council Participation

**6.1.** Sponsor may nominate [NUMBER] representative(s) for non-voting seat(s) on AI-AA's Advisory Council, subject to Board approval.

**6.2.** Sponsor Advisory Council representatives:

- Are clearly designated as sponsor-affiliated in all AI-AA communications
- Must recuse themselves from discussions where Sponsor's direct interests are at stake
- May not participate in the development of policy positions, legal filings, or regulatory recommendations
- Provide input on industry trends, technical capabilities, and implementation considerations only
- Have no governance authority over AI-AA

**6.3.** AI-AA reserves the right to revoke Advisory Council seats if a Sponsor representative acts inconsistently with the terms of this Agreement or the Sponsor Governance Policy.

### Section 7 — Conflict of Interest

**7.1.** Sponsor acknowledges AI-AA's Conflict of Interest Policy and agrees that Sponsor representatives participating in any AI-AA advisory capacity are subject to its disclosure and recusal requirements.

**7.2.** Sponsor shall promptly disclose to AI-AA any material change in circumstances that could create a conflict of interest, including but not limited to: acquisition of or by a company that is a subject of AI-AA analysis, litigation involving disability accessibility claims, or regulatory proceedings related to AI-AA's mission areas.

### Section 8 — Public Disclosure and Transparency

**8.1.** AI-AA will publicly disclose the identity and sponsorship tier of all Sponsors on its website and in its annual report. Specific contribution amounts may be disclosed in aggregate or individually, at AI-AA's sole discretion.

**8.2.** Any AI-AA publication that analyzes, references, or is relevant to Sponsor's products or practices shall include a disclosure noting the sponsorship relationship.

**8.3.** Sponsor agrees to this disclosure regime and shall not require confidentiality of the sponsorship relationship or its terms.

### Section 9 — Termination

**9.1. Termination by Either Party.** Either party may terminate this Agreement with sixty (60) days' written notice.

**9.2. Termination for Cause by AI-AA.** AI-AA may terminate this Agreement immediately if Sponsor:

- Attempts to condition funding on AI-AA's positions or actions (Section 4.2)
- Attempts to influence AI-AA's enforcement decisions or policy positions (Section 4.4)
- Materially breaches any governance provision of this Agreement (Sections 4-8)
- Engages in conduct that, in the Board's judgment, is incompatible with AI-AA's mission

**9.3. Effect of Termination.** Upon termination:

- Unpaid contributions for completed quarters remain due
- Sponsor's Advisory Council seats are revoked
- Sponsor must cease use of "AI-AA Sponsor" designation within thirty (30) days
- AI-AA may publicly disclose the termination and the reason, if the Board determines disclosure is necessary to protect AI-AA's credibility

**9.4. No Refunds.** Contributions made prior to termination are non-refundable, as they constitute charitable donations.

### Section 10 — General Provisions

**10.1. Entire Agreement.** This Agreement, together with the Sponsor Governance Policy and Conflict of Interest Policy (incorporated by reference), constitutes the entire agreement between the parties.

**10.2. Amendment.** This Agreement may be amended only by written agreement signed by both parties. However, AI-AA's governance policies (Sponsor Governance Policy, Conflict of Interest Policy, Bylaws) may be amended by the Board without Sponsor consent.

**10.3. Governing Law.** This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of Delaware.

**10.4. Assignment.** Sponsor may not assign this Agreement without AI-AA's prior written consent. In the event of Sponsor's merger, acquisition, or reorganization, AI-AA may review and, at its discretion, terminate or renegotiate this Agreement.

## Usage Notes for Sponsorships Director

1. **Sections 1-3** are customizable per sponsor and tier. Adjust benefits, payment terms, and tier levels as appropriate.
2. **Sections 4-9** are non-negotiable governance terms derived from the [[Sponsor Governance Policy]]. Do not modify without Operations Director and Board approval.
3. **Section 3.2** has two versions — use the appropriate one depending on whether AI-AA has its own 501(c)(3) status or is operating under fiscal sponsorship.
4. **This template requires legal review before use.** It is drafted to operationalize the Sponsor Governance Policy but has not been reviewed by counsel.
5. **Operations Director must review** each completed agreement for governance compliance before execution (per Sponsor Governance Policy Section 5).
