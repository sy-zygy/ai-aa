# Bylaws of the AI Access Alliance

**Status:** DRAFT v0.1 | **Last Updated:** 2026-04-05 | **Owner:** Operations Director

*These bylaws govern the internal affairs of AI Access Alliance, a [State] nonprofit corporation organized exclusively for charitable and educational purposes under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code.*

---

## Article I — Name and Purpose

### Section 1.1 — Name
The name of the corporation shall be **AI Access Alliance** (hereinafter "the Organization" or "AI-AA").

### Section 1.2 — Purpose
The Organization is organized and operated exclusively for charitable and educational purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code, including but not limited to:

1. Advancing equitable access to artificial intelligence for people with disabilities and neurodivergent individuals
2. Developing and publishing legal and policy frameworks that extend disability-rights protections to AI systems
3. Conducting and publishing research on the impacts of AI on disabled and neurodivergent people
4. Educating the public, policymakers, and industry on accessible AI design and deployment
5. Building coalitions among disability-rights organizations, technology companies, and policymakers to promote inclusive AI

### Section 1.3 — Nondiscrimination
The Organization shall not discriminate on the basis of race, color, national origin, religion, sex, gender identity, sexual orientation, age, or disability in any of its activities or operations.

### Section 1.4 — Limitation on Activities
No substantial part of the activities of the Organization shall be carrying on propaganda or otherwise attempting to influence legislation, and the Organization shall not participate in or intervene in any political campaign on behalf of or in opposition to any candidate for public office.

---

## Article II — Membership

### Section 2.1 — No Members
The Organization shall have no voting members within the meaning of the state nonprofit corporation act. The Board of Directors shall be the sole governing body.

### Section 2.2 — Advisory Council
The Organization may establish an Advisory Council as described in Article VIII. Advisory Council members are not members of the corporation and have no governance authority.

---

## Article III — Board of Directors

### Section 3.1 — General Powers
The affairs of the Organization shall be managed by its Board of Directors ("the Board"). The Board shall have all powers and duties permitted by law and these Bylaws.

### Section 3.2 — Number and Composition
The Board shall consist of no fewer than five (5) and no more than eleven (11) Directors. The target composition is seven to nine (7-9) Directors, allocated across the following seat categories:

| Seat Category | Target Count | Purpose |
|---------------|-------------|---------|
| Disability rights leaders | 2-3 | Lived experience and community legitimacy |
| Legal/policy experts | 2 | ADA, disability law, civil rights expertise |
| Accessibility technology practitioners | 1-2 | Technical credibility and implementation knowledge |
| Non-profit governance / finance experts | 1 | Financial oversight and compliance |
| Founder seat | 1 | Continuity of vision and mission |

**Composition requirement:** At all times, at least one-third (1/3) of seated Directors must be individuals who self-identify as disabled or neurodivergent. Failure to maintain this threshold shall be reported at the next Board meeting and a recruitment plan to restore compliance shall be adopted within sixty (60) days.

### Section 3.3 — Qualifications
Directors must:
1. Have a demonstrated public commitment to disability rights
2. Not be a current employee of any Founding Sponsor or Corporate Sponsor of the Organization (as defined in the Sponsor Governance Policy)
3. Not hold any financial interest that would create a conflict with the Organization's mission, as determined by the Conflict of Interest Policy
4. Not be a current government official in a role that would create a perceived conflict

### Section 3.4 — Election and Term of Office
1. The initial Board shall be appointed by the Incorporator(s).
2. After the initial Board is seated, new Directors shall be elected by a majority vote of the existing Board.
3. Directors shall serve staggered terms of three (3) years, with approximately one-third of seats expiring each year.
4. For the initial Board, terms shall be staggered as follows: one-third shall serve one-year terms, one-third shall serve two-year terms, and one-third shall serve three-year terms, as determined by lot.
5. No Director may serve more than two (2) consecutive full terms. After a gap of at least one (1) year, a former Director may be re-elected.

### Section 3.5 — Vacancies
Vacancies on the Board may be filled by a majority vote of the remaining Directors. A Director appointed to fill a vacancy shall serve for the remainder of the unexpired term.

### Section 3.6 — Removal
A Director may be removed with or without cause by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the Board. The Director subject to removal shall be given at least fourteen (14) days' written notice and the opportunity to be heard before the Board votes.

### Section 3.7 — Resignation
A Director may resign at any time by submitting written notice to the Board Chair. Resignation takes effect upon receipt or at a later date specified in the notice.

### Section 3.8 — Compensation
Directors shall not receive compensation for their service on the Board. Directors may be reimbursed for reasonable expenses incurred in the performance of Board duties, as approved by the Treasurer.

---

## Article IV — Meetings of the Board

### Section 4.1 — Regular Meetings
The Board shall hold at least four (4) regular meetings per calendar year, at least one of which shall be an in-person or hybrid meeting. Regular meetings may be conducted in person, by video conference, or by telephone conference. The schedule of regular meetings shall be established annually at the first meeting of the calendar year.

### Section 4.2 — Annual Meeting
The Board shall hold an Annual Meeting during the first quarter of each calendar year for the purpose of electing officers, reviewing the Organization's finances and activities, and conducting other business.

### Section 4.3 — Special Meetings
Special meetings may be called by the Board Chair, the Executive Director, or any three (3) Directors. Written notice shall be given to all Directors at least seven (7) days before a special meeting, stating the purpose of the meeting.

### Section 4.4 — Quorum
A majority of the seated Directors shall constitute a quorum for the transaction of business. No business shall be conducted without a quorum present.

### Section 4.5 — Voting
1. Each Director shall have one (1) vote.
2. Unless otherwise specified in these Bylaws, all actions of the Board shall require a majority vote of the Directors present at a meeting where a quorum exists.
3. Proxy voting is not permitted.

### Section 4.6 — Action Without Meeting
The Board may take action without a meeting if all Directors consent in writing (including electronic consent) to the action. Written consents shall be filed with the minutes of the Board.

### Section 4.7 — Minutes
Minutes shall be kept for all Board meetings and shall be distributed to all Directors within fourteen (14) days of each meeting.

---

## Article V — Officers

### Section 5.1 — Officers
The officers of the Organization shall be:
1. **Board Chair** — Presides over Board meetings, sets meeting agendas, serves as primary liaison between Board and Executive Director
2. **Vice Chair** — Acts in the Board Chair's absence, assists with governance oversight
3. **Secretary** — Maintains corporate records, ensures proper notice of meetings, oversees minutes
4. **Treasurer** — Oversees financial affairs, reviews financial statements, presents financial reports to the Board

### Section 5.2 — Election and Term
Officers shall be elected by the Board at the Annual Meeting. Officers shall serve one-year terms and may be re-elected without term limits.

### Section 5.3 — Removal
Any officer may be removed by a majority vote of the Board at any regular or special meeting.

### Section 5.4 — Vacancies
Officer vacancies shall be filled by the Board for the remainder of the unexpired term.

### Section 5.5 — Executive Director
The Board may appoint an Executive Director who shall serve at the pleasure of the Board and be responsible for the day-to-day management of the Organization. The Executive Director shall be a non-voting, ex-officio member of the Board.

---

## Article VI — Committees

### Section 6.1 — Standing Committees
The Board shall establish the following standing committees:

1. **Governance Committee** — Oversees Board composition, nominations, bylaws compliance, and governance policies. Minimum three (3) Directors.
2. **Finance & Audit Committee** — Oversees financial management, budget, audit, and fiscal policies. Chaired by the Treasurer. Minimum three (3) Directors.
3. **Programs Committee** — Reviews and provides guidance on programmatic activities (policy, research, education). Minimum two (2) Directors.

### Section 6.2 — Ad Hoc Committees
The Board Chair may create ad hoc committees for specific purposes, with Board approval. Ad hoc committees shall have a defined scope and expiration date.

### Section 6.3 — Committee Authority
Committees may make recommendations to the Board but may not take binding action on behalf of the Organization unless specifically delegated by Board resolution.

### Section 6.4 — Committee Membership
Committee members need not be Directors, except that each standing committee shall be chaired by a Director and have a majority of Directors as members. Non-Director committee members shall have no vote on Board matters.

---

## Article VII — Sponsor Governance Firewall

### Section 7.1 — Incorporation by Reference
The Organization's Sponsor Governance Policy, as adopted and amended by the Board, is incorporated into these Bylaws by reference. All provisions of the Sponsor Governance Policy shall have the same force as if set forth in full in these Bylaws.

### Section 7.2 — Core Provisions
The following provisions may not be waived or amended except by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the full Board:

1. **No sponsor votes.** No corporate sponsor, sponsor representative, or sponsor-affiliated individual shall hold a voting seat on the Board.
2. **No sponsor veto.** No sponsor shall have veto power over the Organization's legal positions, policy recommendations, enforcement targets, or litigation decisions.
3. **No pre-publication review.** Sponsors shall not receive advance access to policy positions, legal filings, or research publications prior to public release.
4. **No influence over enforcement.** Sponsors shall not influence decisions about which companies or products are subjects of policy criticism, legal analysis, or regulatory complaints.

### Section 7.3 — Super-Majority Requirement
Any amendment to this Article VII or to the Sponsor Governance Policy shall require a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the full Board (not merely those present at a meeting).

---

## Article VIII — Advisory Council

### Section 8.1 — Purpose
The Advisory Council provides subject-matter expertise and community accountability to the Organization. Advisory Council members advise the Board and staff but have no governance authority.

### Section 8.2 — Composition
The Advisory Council shall consist of twelve to fifteen (12-15) members, drawn from:
- Disabled and neurodivergent community leaders (4-5 members)
- Legal scholars in disability and civil rights law (2-3 members)
- Accessibility practitioners and researchers (2-3 members)
- AI researchers (1-2 members)
- Sponsor representatives (2-3 members, non-voting, advisory only)

### Section 8.3 — Appointment and Terms
Advisory Council members shall be appointed by the Board for two-year terms, renewable without limit. Members may be removed by a majority vote of the Board.

### Section 8.4 — Sponsor Representatives
Sponsor representatives on the Advisory Council:
1. Are clearly designated as sponsor-affiliated in all communications
2. Must recuse themselves from discussions where their employer's interests are directly at stake
3. May not participate in the development of policy positions, legal filings, or regulatory recommendations
4. Serve in an advisory capacity only with no governance authority

### Section 8.5 — Meetings
The Advisory Council shall meet at least twice per year. Meetings may be conducted in person, by video conference, or by telephone.

---

## Article IX — Conflict of Interest

### Section 9.1 — Incorporation by Reference
The Organization's Conflict of Interest Policy, as adopted and amended by the Board, is incorporated into these Bylaws by reference.

### Section 9.2 — Duty to Disclose
All Directors, officers, and key employees shall disclose any actual or potential conflict of interest in accordance with the Conflict of Interest Policy.

### Section 9.3 — Annual Certification
All Directors and officers shall complete an annual conflict-of-interest disclosure and certification.

---

## Article X — Fiscal Matters

### Section 10.1 — Fiscal Year
The fiscal year of the Organization shall be January 1 through December 31.

### Section 10.2 — Financial Review
The Board shall ensure that an independent financial review or audit is conducted annually, as appropriate for the Organization's size and revenue.

### Section 10.3 — Signing Authority
Checks, contracts, and other financial instruments shall require signatures as specified by Board resolution. No single individual shall have sole authority over disbursements exceeding a threshold set by the Finance & Audit Committee.

---

## Article XI — Indemnification

### Section 11.1 — Indemnification of Directors and Officers
The Organization shall indemnify its Directors, officers, employees, and agents to the fullest extent permitted by law, provided they acted in good faith and in a manner they reasonably believed to be in the best interests of the Organization.

### Section 11.2 — Insurance
The Organization shall obtain and maintain Directors and Officers (D&O) liability insurance in an amount deemed adequate by the Board.

---

## Article XII — Amendments

### Section 12.1 — General Amendments
These Bylaws may be amended by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the Directors present at a meeting where a quorum exists, provided that written notice of the proposed amendment has been given to all Directors at least fourteen (14) days before the meeting.

### Section 12.2 — Protected Provisions
Amendments to Article VII (Sponsor Governance Firewall) or to the composition requirements in Section 3.2 require a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the full Board (not merely those present).

---

## Article XIII — Dissolution

### Section 13.1 — Dissolution
Upon dissolution, the assets of the Organization shall be distributed for one or more exempt purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code, or to the federal government or a state or local government for a public purpose. Preference shall be given to organizations serving disabled and neurodivergent communities.
