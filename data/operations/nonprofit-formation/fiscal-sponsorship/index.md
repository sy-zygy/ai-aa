# Fiscal Sponsorship — Bridge to 501(c)(3)

**Status:** Research | **Owner:** Operations Director | **Urgency:** High

## Why We Need This

AI-AA's 501(c)(3) application (Form 1023) will take 3-6 months to process after filing. During this period:
- Corporate sponsors need tax-deductible donation receipts
- Foundation grants typically require 501(c)(3) status or fiscal sponsorship
- We cannot legally represent ourselves as tax-exempt without a determination letter

A **fiscal sponsor** is an existing 501(c)(3) organization that receives tax-deductible donations on behalf of a sponsored project. The fiscal sponsor provides its tax-exempt status, handles donation receipts, and passes through funds minus an administrative fee (typically 5-10%).

## Fiscal Sponsorship Models

### Model A — Comprehensive (Direct Project)
The fiscal sponsor treats AI-AA as an internal project. The sponsor has legal control and fiduciary responsibility. Staff may be employed by the sponsor.

- **Pros:** Maximum legal protection, simplest for donors
- **Cons:** Less autonomy for AI-AA, sponsor has ultimate authority over funds
- **Best for:** Very early-stage projects without their own board

### Model C — Pre-Approved Grant Relationship
The fiscal sponsor makes grants to AI-AA as a separate entity. AI-AA maintains its own legal identity (once incorporated) and operational independence. The sponsor verifies that funds are used for charitable purposes.

- **Pros:** AI-AA retains operational independence, own board governs, cleaner separation
- **Cons:** Slightly more complex, sponsor must approve each grant distribution
- **Best for:** AI-AA's situation — we will have our own incorporation and board, just awaiting IRS determination

**Recommendation: Model C.** AI-AA should be incorporated (Delaware Articles) before entering a fiscal sponsorship. Model C preserves our governance independence — critical given our firewall commitments to sponsors.

## Cost

Fiscal sponsorship administrative fees are typically:
- **5-7%** of funds received (Social Good Fund, Community Initiatives)
- **7-10%** of funds received (Tides, New Venture Fund)
- **Negotiable** for mission-aligned orgs (DREDF — may waive or reduce)

On $500K in Year 1 funding, a 7% fee = $35K. Significant but necessary.

## Governance Implications

The fiscal sponsorship agreement must preserve AI-AA's governance independence:
- Fiscal sponsor does NOT gain board seats or policy influence
- AI-AA's Sponsor Governance Policy applies to the fiscal sponsor relationship
- AI-AA's board (once seated) retains authority over programmatic decisions
- The fiscal sponsor's role is limited to financial pass-through and compliance oversight

**Operations Director must review the fiscal sponsorship agreement for governance firewall consistency before execution.**
