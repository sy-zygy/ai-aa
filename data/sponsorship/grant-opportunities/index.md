## Strategic Notes

### Grants vs. Corporate Sponsorships

Grants and corporate sponsorships serve different functions:

- **Corporate sponsorships** provide unrestricted funding, market validation, and strategic relationships. They are the primary revenue engine.
- **Grants** provide project-specific funding, foundation credibility, and diversified revenue. They also serve as **warm intro pathways** to corporate sponsors (e.g., a Google.org grant creates a direct relationship with Google's social impact team).

### Dual-Purpose Applications

Several grant programs overlap with sponsor targets. Applying for grants from Microsoft, Google, OpenAI, and Meta creates touchpoints with their social impact teams — even if the grant isn't awarded, the application process introduces AI-AA to decision-makers.

### 501(c)(3) Requirement

Most foundation grants require 501(c)(3) status or fiscal sponsorship. **Operations Director dependency:** confirm 501(c)(3) timeline or identify fiscal sponsor before submitting applications.

### HHS Section 1557 Context

The HHS final rule on nondiscrimination (Section 1557) includes provisions on AI in healthcare effective 2024-2026. This creates institutional demand for the exact guidance AI-AA produces — model policies for AI comprehension tools in healthcare settings. This regulatory context strengthens every grant application in the healthcare accessibility space.
