# Sponsorship & Funding

**Owner:** Sponsorships Director | **Status:** Active | **Updated:** 2026-04-06

> **Sprint status:** Pitch deck COMPLETE (v1.0). Microsoft and Anthropic outreach emails DRAFTED — awaiting CEO review. Sponsor agreement template with governance firewall CONFIRMED (Operations delivered Sections 4-9 non-negotiable). Fiscal sponsorship via DREDF approved by CEO.

The Sponsorships department is AI-AA's revenue engine. Without funding, the doctrine memo gathers dust, the coalition never forms, and the mission dies. This department turns AI-AA's compelling mission into committed dollars — and ensures sponsors understand they are funding a disability-rights initiative, not buying influence over it.

## Department Scope

### What's In Scope

- Corporate sponsor identification, research, outreach, and acquisition
- Grant identification and application (foundations, government grants)
- Donor relations and stewardship
- Pitch materials creation and maintenance (decks, proposals, one-pagers)
- Sponsor pipeline tracking and management
- Sponsor agreement negotiation (with governance firewall provisions)
- Impact reporting to sponsors
- Individual donor strategy (future phase)

### What's Out of Scope

- Legal analysis of sponsor governance provisions (that's Policy + Operations)
- Public announcements of sponsor partnerships (that's Comms — we coordinate)
- Board seat allocation for sponsors (that's Operations — we advise)
- Content creation beyond pitch materials (that's Comms)

## The Core Pitch

**One-line:** "You fund accessibility. We turn that into civil-rights recognition."

**Three-sentence:** "Your company already invests in making AI accessible and beneficial. But there's no legal or policy infrastructure that recognizes AI comprehension tools as the auxiliary aids they've become. AI Access Alliance builds that infrastructure — the doctrine, evidence, coalitions, and institutional standards — so your accessibility investment produces durable, institutional impact."

## Operating Model

### How This Department Works

- **Relationship-first.** Never lead with the ask. Lead with mission, stories, evidence. The ask comes after alignment.
- **Customize everything.** No generic pitches. Every outreach references the target's specific programs and strategic interests.
- **Track religiously.** Maintain a pipeline with stages, next actions, and owners. Update after every interaction.
- **Respect the firewall.** Never promise sponsors influence over legal positions or policy recommendations. Non-negotiable.
- **Move fast on warm leads.** Corporate budget cycles are slow. Start outreach now for commitments 3-6 months out.

### Outreach Sequence

1. **Research** — Deep-dive on each target's existing accessibility/AI programs
2. **Warm introduction** — Identify connections through networks, conferences, mutual contacts
3. **Initial pitch** — Tailored 1-page executive summary + customized deck
4. **Deep dive** — Full proposal with budget, milestones, governance structure
5. **Negotiation** — Sponsorship agreement with governance firewall provisions
6. **Close** — Signed agreement, press announcement, onboarding

### Cross-Department Coordination

- **With Research:** Request fresh evidence, user stories, and impact data for each pitch. Stories featuring a sponsor's products as comprehension aids are especially valuable.
- **With Communications:** Coordinate sponsor announcements. Align on messaging for sponsor-visible materials.
- **With Policy:** Doctrine memo is the anchor document for pitches. Technical standards are a key sponsor deliverable.
- **With Operations:** All sponsor agreements must include governance firewall provisions. Review with Operations before finalizing.

### The Governance Firewall

This is the credibility insurance that makes sponsor fundraising viable without compromising mission integrity:

- Sponsors receive **no votes** on the board
- Sponsors have **no veto** over legal positions or policy recommendations
- Sponsors get **no access** to pre-publication policy drafts
- Sponsors have **no influence** over enforcement targets or litigation decisions
- Sponsor agreements **expressly state** these restrictions

If this firewall is ever perceived as compromised, AI-AA's credibility collapses. Protect it.
