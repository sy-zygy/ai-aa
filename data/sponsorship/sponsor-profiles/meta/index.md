## 2. Ray-Ban Meta Smart Glasses — Accessibility

Ray-Ban Meta glasses are Meta's most accessibility-relevant hardware product and the centerpiece of an AI-AA sponsorship pitch. The glasses are manufactured in partnership with EssilorLuxottica.

### Sales and Adoption

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Cumulative units sold** | 2+ million (as of Feb 2025) |
| **2024 units sold** | ~1 million |
| **Revenue growth** | Tripled YoY in H1 2025 |
| **2026 production target** | 10 million units/year (EssilorLuxottica goal) |
| **Starting price** | $299 (Ray-Ban Meta); $799 (Meta Ray-Ban Display + Neural Band) |

### AI Features Relevant to Accessibility

**Look and Ask / Meta AI Vision:**
- Users ask Meta AI to describe what's in front of them via voice command ("Hey Meta, look and tell me...")
- Describes scenes, reads text, identifies objects, translates signs
- Available in US and Canada; expanding to additional markets

**Live AI:**
- Continuous conversational AI that sees what you see throughout a session
- Help with everyday activities: meal planning, navigation, city exploration
- Available in English, rolling out in US and Canada

**Live Captions (Meta Ray-Ban Display):**
- Real-time text transcription of speech displayed on the lens
- Captions appear discreetly in the display, allowing wearers to maintain eye contact
- First mainstream consumer product with built-in captioning in a stylish form factor

**Live Translation (Meta Ray-Ban Display):**
- Real-time translation across six languages: English, Spanish, French, Italian, German, Portuguese
- Offline language packs available for airplane mode
- Written translation appears on display during conversations

**Conversation Focus:**
- AI-powered speech enhancement that isolates conversation in noisy environments
- Described as potential hearing aid complement

### Accessibility Use Cases by Disability

**Blind / Low Vision:**
- Scene description via "Look and Ask" provides environmental awareness
- Text reading via OCR for documents, signs, menus, labels
- Be My Eyes integration for hands-free volunteer assistance
- AIRA integration for professional visual interpretation
- AFB review (Fall 2025): "For the price and convenience, they offer a great deal in object recognition, environmental description, and reading with OCR"
- Consumer Reports and multiple disability organizations have reviewed positively

**Deaf / Hard of Hearing:**
- Live captions on Display model — real-time speech-to-text on lens
- Live translation — cross-language captioning
- Zuckerberg quote: "If you have an issue hearing, then I think that this is gonna be a game changer"
- First consumer-grade glasses with built-in captioning that doesn't require a phone

**DeafBlind:**
- Helen Keller Services review (May 2025, by Scott Davert): Mixed results
- "Look and Tell" can be activated via text on connected braille display after app update
- Critical limitation: Audio cannot be routed to hearing aids, cochlear implants, or external devices
- Voice requirement for most features excludes non-speaking users
- Assessment: "For those with hearing loss, the Meta Ray-Ban smart glasses may not be ready for prime time"

**Cognitive / Reading Disabilities:**
- Meta AI can simplify and explain complex text read via camera
- Translation features assist users who comprehend better in another language
- Scene description reduces cognitive load of environmental processing

### Meta Neural Band

- EMG (electromyography) wristband that translates muscle signals into commands
- Subtle finger movements control the glasses without voice or touch
- Accessibility potential: Can work for users who "can't produce large movements (due to a spinal cord injury, stroke or another disabling event), experience tremors, or have fewer than five fingers"
- Active research with Carnegie Mellon University on spinal cord injury accessibility
- Clinical Research Organization evaluating tremor users (Parkinson's, Essential Tremor)
- Bosworth acknowledged the team "hasn't yet done work to understand" prosthetic limb scenarios

### Key Caveat

The AFB review noted: "The Ray-Ban Meta glasses are an accessibility tool only by coincidence... the fact that some of the features happen to be handy for people with vision loss is purely by chance." This means Meta has accessibility impact without accessibility intent — AI-AA could help them formalize this.

## 5. Meta AI as Comprehension Aid

### Meta AI Assistant

Meta AI is deployed across Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, Messenger, and Ray-Ban Meta glasses — reaching 3.58 billion daily active people. It functions as a de facto comprehension aid through:

| Function | How It Works |
|----------|-------------|
| **Text summarization** | Summarize long posts, articles, messages |
| **Language simplification** | Rephrase complex content in simpler terms |
| **Translation** | Real-time translation across multiple languages |
| **Visual description** | Describe images, scenes, and text in photos |
| **Q&A** | Answer clarifying questions about content |
| **Document reading** | Read and explain text captured by glasses camera |

### Llama Models — Accessibility Applications

**Scale:** 1.2+ billion downloads across all Llama versions, making it the most adopted open AI model on the planet.

**Healthcare applications using Llama:**
- Zauron Labs Guardian AI: Double-checks radiological imaging to find errors
- Mendel Hypercube: Chat-based patient data insights for trial matching
- Saama: Clinical trial protocol generation and data analysis
- Me-LLaMA: Open-source medical LLM family for medical text analysis
- Meditron (Yale/EPFL): Emergency medicine and underserved area healthcare support

**Accessibility-specific Llama applications:**
- **Sign-Speak + Llama:** WhatsApp chatbot translating ASL to English text (and vice versa via avatar). Deaf person signs to camera, hearing person types/speaks — bidirectional communication.
- **Llama 4 multimodal:** Can read documents, understand images, summarize conversations, answer visual questions — all comprehension aid functions
- **10M token context window (Scout):** Multi-document summarization at scale

**Government deployment:**
- **GSA OneGov partnership** (September 2025): Llama models available free to all federal agencies
- Supports OMB memoranda on accelerating federal AI use
- Federal agencies can build, deploy, and scale AI applications at no cost

### AI-AA Relevance

Meta AI's deployment across 3.58 billion users means that when institutions block AI tools, they potentially block comprehension aids used by the largest user base on earth. The GSA partnership means the federal government itself uses Llama — AI-AA's doctrine about AI as auxiliary aids in government settings directly applies.

Two million pairs sold, Be My Eyes integrated, live captions on the Display — but the AFB says your glasses are 'an accessibility tool only by coincidence.' We build the legal doctrine and institutional standards that formally recognize AI wearables as auxiliary aids under existing disability law — so when a school, hospital, or government agency makes technology decisions, your glasses aren't an afterthought. They're a protected accessibility tool. You built the hardware. We build the legal recognition it deserves."
