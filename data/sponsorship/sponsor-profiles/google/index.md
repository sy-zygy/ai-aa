## 1. Company Overview

Google (Alphabet Inc.) is the world's dominant search and advertising company, with major platforms including Android (3B+ devices), Chrome, YouTube, Google Workspace, and Google Cloud. The company has made accessibility a company-wide priority under its "Products for All" initiative, led by Senior Director Eve Andersson. Google.org, the philanthropic arm led by VP/Global Head Maggie Johnson, has contributed nearly $6B in cash to nonprofits and over $18B in product donations.

## 4. Google for Education Accessibility

### Read Along App
- **What:** AI speech-based reading tutor that helps children learn to read by reading aloud
- **Origin:** Launched in India as "Bolo," now available globally
- **AI Features:** Built-in reading assistant "Diya" provides real-time verbal and visual feedback; comprehension questions as students progress
- **Impact:** Studies show 42% improvement in Hindi reading fluency, 20% in Marathi; 81% improvement in confidence; 83% improvement in reading interest
- **2025 Update:** New silent reading mode added (April 2025) so students can read without drawing attention or disturbing classmates
- **Integration:** Available in Google Classroom
- **Accessibility:** Works offline, available on Android and web

### Google Workspace for Education Accessibility Features
- **Accessibility fonts:** Andika, Lexend, and Atkinson Hyperlegible added to Docs, Sheets, Slides
- **Select-to-Speak:** Highlights text and reads aloud (useful for dyslexia, ELL students)
- **Voice typing:** Built into Google Docs
- **Reading mode in Chrome:** Enlarges text, reduces distractions; "Read Aloud" feature with 100+ natural voices across 29 languages
- **Gemini integration:** Summarize long texts for overwhelmed students; adapt materials to different reading levels; generate audio-friendly scripts
- **Grammar/Spell Check:** Automated error correction supporting ADHD, dyslexia focus
- **Face Control:** Navigate screens using facial gestures and head movements (Chromebook)

### Chromebook Accessibility
- **ChromeVox:** Built-in screen reader with braille display support (USB/Bluetooth)
- **Select-to-Speak:** Visual word highlighting while reading aloud
- **Reading mode + Read Aloud:** Natural voices, distraction-free reading
- **Screen magnifier, high contrast, sticky keys** built in

### Google.org Education Grants for Disability
- **CSEveryone:** ~$1M grant (Oct 2025) for inclusive CS/AI education for K-12 students with disabilities; expanding from Broward County and Chicago to national credentials
- **Tech Kids Unlimited:** ~$250K grant for digitizing free teacher portal with tech/career courses for neurodiverse learners (NYC)
- **Blockly Accessibility Fund:** $5M for organizations developing accessible block-based coding tools
- **Total education accessibility:** $1.2M+ announced October 2025 (National Disability Employment Awareness Month)

### Partnership with Understood.org
- **Longstanding relationship** to improve tools for neurodivergent students
- **"Through My Eyes"** (launched September 9, 2025): First-of-its-kind interactive platform co-created with Google
  - 9 documentary-style videos of kids with ADHD, dyslexia, dyscalculia
  - 6 gamified simulations of daily scenarios
  - 16 expert-created resources (self-advocacy packs, lesson plans, co-parenting tips)
  - Free on mobile, tablet, desktop, Chromebooks
- **Global Forum on Neuroinclusion:** Co-hosted with partner Everway in New York

### Project Relate
- **What:** Android app for people with non-standard speech (250M+ people worldwide affected)
- **How:** Users record phrases; app builds personalized speech recognition model
- **Three features:**
  - **Listen:** Real-time speech-to-text transcription
  - **Repeat:** Restates speech using clear synthesized voice (for face-to-face or home assistant)
  - **Assistant:** Speak directly to Google Assistant
- **Availability:** English speakers 18+ in Australia, Canada, Ghana, India, New Zealand, UK, US
- **Beta:** Launched in UK

### Google Lookout
- **What:** Computer vision + generative AI app for blind/low-vision users
- **Seven modes:** Text, Documents, Explore, Currency, Food Labels, Find, Images
- **Key capabilities:**
  - Find mode: direction and distance to objects (doors, bathrooms, cups, vehicles)
  - Text/Documents: 30+ languages, works offline (on-device processing)
  - Images: Capture, describe, and ask questions about images
- **Status:** Actively maintained and regularly updated

### TalkBack + Gemini Integration
- **2024:** Gemini-powered AI image descriptions added to TalkBack (Android's screen reader)
- **2025 GAAD expansion:**
  - Users can now ask follow-up questions about images
  - Descriptions of entire screen (not just individual images)
  - Uses Gemini Nano (on-device) + Gemini 1.5 Flash (cloud) hybrid approach
- **Impact:** Average TalkBack user encounters 90 unlabeled images per day; early testers call it a "game changer"

### Expressive Captions
- **What:** AI-powered captions that capture not just what someone says but how they say it
- **Features:**
  - Capitalization for intensity ("HAPPY BIRTHDAY!")
  - Vocal bursts (sighing, grunting, gasping)
  - Ambient sounds (applause, cheers)
  - Emotion tags: [joyful], [sad], [angry]
  - Duration feature: "amaaazing" vs "amazing"
- **2025 rollout:** English in US, UK, Canada, Australia; Android 15+

### Other Accessibility Products
- **Live Transcribe:** Real-time speech-to-text in 70+ languages; two-way conversation via type-back keyboard
- **Sound Amplifier:** Filters, augments, amplifies environmental audio
- **Look to Speak:** Eye-tracking communication app; 2024 update added text-free mode with customizable phrases, emojis, symbols, photos (for cognitive differences, literacy challenges, language barriers)
- **Sound Notifications:** Alerts for important household sounds

## 6. Google AI Accessibility Research

### Project Euphonia
- **What:** Research initiative for speech recognition for people with non-standard speech
- **Launched:** 2019
- **Results:** Reduced median word error rates by 80%+ for non-standard speech
- **2024 expansion:** Extended to Africa (Ghana) via partnership with University of Ghana and UCL's Global Disability Innovation Hub (GDI Hub); expanding to Spanish, Japanese, French, Hindi
- **Published research:** "Project Euphonia: Advancing Inclusive Speech Recognition through Expanded Data Collection and Evaluation" — Frontiers in Language Sciences (2025)
- **Status:** No longer collecting speech samples; sharing learnings and data for ecosystem adoption

### Speech Accessibility Project
- **What:** Cross-industry initiative led by University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign
- **Partners:** Google, Apple, Amazon, Meta, Microsoft
- **Focus:** Diverse speech data for people with Parkinson's, Down syndrome, ALS, cerebral palsy, stroke
- **Progress (Sept 2025):** ~2,000 participants have submitted recordings
- **Data sharing:** De-identified data being sent to corporate sponsors and other researchers/companies/universities
- **Impact:** Microsoft reported 18-60% accuracy gains using the data

### Published Research Areas
- Personalized speech recognition for non-standard speech
- AI-generated image descriptions (Gemini + TalkBack)
- Expressive captioning with emotional tone detection
- On-device ML for accessibility (Gemini Nano)

## 10. Alignment Analysis — Why AI-AA Fits Google

### Budget Lines We Align With
1. **Google.org AI Opportunity Fund** ($120M global) — AI-AA trains people with cognitive disabilities to use AI as auxiliary aids
2. **Google.org Generative AI Accelerator** ($30M/cohort) — AI-AA could be a cohort participant
3. **Accessibility programs budget** (undisclosed but substantial) — AI-AA produces the policy framework that gives Google's accessibility products institutional recognition
4. **Google for Education accessibility** — AI-AA's doctrine positions Google's reading tools (Read Along, Gemini summarization, Select-to-Speak) as legally recognized auxiliary aids

### Products That Function as Comprehension Aids
These Google products already serve as de facto auxiliary aids for people with cognitive disabilities. AI-AA's work would give them legal/institutional recognition:

| Product | Comprehension Function |
|---|---|
| **Read Along** | AI reading tutor with real-time feedback and comprehension questions |
| **Gemini in Workspace** | Text summarization, reading level adaptation, audio-friendly scripts |
| **Select-to-Speak** | Visual word tracking + audio for reading difficulties |
| **Chrome Reading Mode + Read Aloud** | Distraction-free reading with natural voice narration |
| **Lookout** | Document reading and scene description for low-vision users |
| **TalkBack + Gemini** | AI image descriptions and interactive Q&A for blind users |
| **Live Transcribe** | Real-time speech-to-text for hearing-impaired comprehension |
| **Expressive Captions** | Emotional/tonal context in captions for fuller comprehension |
| **Project Relate** | Speech recognition for non-standard speech enabling communication |
| **Look to Speak** | Eye-tracking communication for people with cognitive/motor differences |
| **Accessibility fonts (Lexend, Atkinson Hyperlegible)** | Improved readability for dyslexia and low vision |

### Strategic Fit
- Google already invests heavily in AI accessibility but lacks the **policy/legal infrastructure** to get these tools recognized as auxiliary aids in regulated settings (education, employment, government)
- AI-AA provides the **doctrine, evidence, and institutional standards** that convert Google's product investments into durable civil-rights impact
- Google's partnership with Understood.org (neurodivergent students) and NFB (blind users) shows willingness to work with advocacy organizations
- Google's open-sourcing requirement from the Impact Challenge: Disabilities aligns with AI-AA's coalition model

### Key Entry Points
1. **Eve Andersson** — Senior Director, Products for All (owns accessibility strategy)
2. **Maggie Johnson** — VP, Google.org (owns philanthropy/grants pipeline)
3. **Angana Ghosh** — Director of Product Management, Android (owns accessibility product roadmap)
4. **Google.org Generative AI Accelerator** — Apply as a cohort participant for funding + Google AI expert support
5. **Accessibility Discovery Centres** — Propose a joint event or research collaboration
6. **Understood.org** — Potential warm introduction pathway (shared mission territory)

## 11. Risks and Objections

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "We already fund accessibility through Google.org" | AI-AA converts your product investments into legal recognition. Without policy infrastructure, your tools remain optional conveniences, not recognized auxiliary aids. |
| "Policy advocacy isn't what we fund" | Google.org funds the AI Opportunity Fund (workforce), education, and community resilience. AI-AA operates at the intersection of all three. |
| "We don't want to be associated with regulatory advocacy" | AI-AA's doctrine is pro-technology. We argue AI tools SHOULD be recognized as aids — this expands Google's addressable market in education, government, and employment. |
| "Governance concerns" | AI-AA's sponsor governance firewall ensures no sponsor influence over mission/policy. Advisory seat only. |
