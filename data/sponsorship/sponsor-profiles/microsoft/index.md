**Tier:** 1 (Founding Sponsor) | **Stage:** Outreach Ready | **Priority:** #1

## 1. Company Overview

Microsoft is the world's largest software company with deep, structural commitments to accessibility spanning products, workforce, and policy. Under President Brad Smith's 2021 five-year plan "Doubling Down on Accessibility," Microsoft committed to treating accessibility as a company-wide priority across three pillars: accessible technology, workforce inclusion, and workplace culture. Chief Accessibility Officer Jenny Lay-Flurrie (who is deaf) has led the company's accessibility vision since 2016.

Microsoft's accessibility investments are not CSR add-ons — they are embedded in core products used by billions (Windows, Office, Teams, Edge, Xbox). This makes Microsoft the single most aligned Tier 1 target for AI-AA: their products already function as comprehension aids, and they have the institutional infrastructure to fund and amplify our mission.

- Multiple cohorts since 2023

## 3. Microsoft Products as AI Comprehension Aids

These products already function as de facto auxiliary aids. AI-AA's work would give them legal and institutional recognition.

### Immersive Reader
- **What it does:** AI-powered reading tool with text-to-speech, syllable highlighting, line focus, picture dictionary, translation (60+ languages), grammar tools, and reading speed controls
- **Deployment:** Built into Word, OneNote, Outlook, Teams, Edge, Forms, Minecraft Education, and Reading Progress
- **User base:** 35 million monthly users (Brad Smith, 2021); 560% usage increase Feb-May 2020 during remote learning
- **Third-party integration:** Reached general availability as an Azure Cognitive Service in April 2025 — partner apps can now embed Immersive Reader
- **AI-AA relevance:** This is the single strongest example of a Microsoft product that IS an auxiliary aid for cognitive accessibility. When schools block AI tools, they may block Immersive Reader functionality.

### Seeing AI
- **Platforms:** iOS (since 2017) and Android (launched December 2023). Free.
- **Capabilities:** 10 channels — Short Text, Documents (with generative AI chat/Q&A), Products, Scenes, People, Currency, Colors, Handwriting, Light, Images from other apps
- **AI features:** Generative AI-powered photo descriptions; document chat for asking questions about scanned text
- **Languages:** 18 currently, expanding to 36
- **Leadership:** Saqib Shaikh, Founder and Lead (blind since age 7)
- **AI-AA relevance:** Seeing AI's document chat is exactly the kind of "user-authorized AI comprehension tool" our doctrine covers

### Copilot Accessibility Features
- **Document comprehension:** Summarizes documents, emails, transcripts into digestible formats
- **Language simplification:** Rewrites complex content into plain language
- **Visual description:** Describes charts, graphs, images, slides on request
- **Assistive tech compatibility:** Works with screen readers, magnifiers, voice input

### EY/Microsoft Copilot Accessibility Study (December 2024)
This is pitch gold — hard data showing AI as assistive technology:
- **300+ employees** across 17 organizations and 7 sectors surveyed
- **91%** consider Copilot a helpful assistive technology
- **88%** feel more productive
- **87%** say it reduces mental energy demanded by tasks
- **85%** believe it creates a more inclusive workplace
- **76%** say it helps them thrive more at work

### Other Comprehension Aid Products

| Product | Function |
|---------|----------|
| **Narrator** | AI-powered image descriptions launching spring 2025; expressive AI voices on Copilot+ PCs |
| **Read Aloud in Edge** | 10+ million monthly users reading web content aloud |
| **Reading Coach** | Standalone school app (April 2025), works alongside Immersive Reader |
| **Speech Accessibility Project** | Partnership with UIUC — 2,000 participants, 400+ hours speech data, 18-60% accuracy improvement for non-standard speech |

| **Teams Sign Language View** | Auto-detects sign language, features signer prominently (2025 improvements) |

### Demographic Context
- 1 in 5 people globally identify as neurodivergent
- 53% of Gen Z identify as neurodivergent
- Gen Alpha projected at 70%+
- This is Microsoft's own framing — it positions accessibility as a massive market, not a niche

## 5. Disability Answer Desk & Disability Programs

### Disability Answer Desk (DAD)
- Free technical support for individuals with disabilities using Microsoft products
- **Enterprise DAD (eDAD):** Support for organizations on product accessibility
- **Be My Eyes integration:** Microsoft was the **first company** to integrate Be My AI into customer service
  - 47% reduction in handle time
  - 5% increase in customer satisfaction
  - Average resolution: 4 minutes (vs. 12 minutes with human agent)

### Workforce Programs
- **Neurodiversity Hiring Program:** Expanded from autism-only to ADHD, dyslexia, dyspraxia. Expanding to Asia and Europe.
- **Autism Employer Hiring Coalition:** Microsoft-led; partners include Dell, EY, JPMorgan Chase
- **Disability self-identification:** 6.1% of U.S. Microsoft employees have self-identified as having a disability; survey expanding to 45 countries
- **Global centralized accommodations budget:** Workplace accommodations are never subject to local team financial pressure
- **Supported Employment Program:** Expanded from 1 to 12 countries
- **Ability Hack:** Employee hackathon with 6,500+ participants since 2014, producing 1,000+ projects

## 8. Alignment Analysis — Why AI-AA Fits Microsoft

### Budget Lines We Align With
1. **AI for Accessibility** ($25M grants) — AI-AA extends the impact of tools this program funds by securing their legal recognition as auxiliary aids
2. **Microsoft Elevate** ($4B+ over 5 years) — AI-AA fits the "nonprofits" and "K-12 schools" focus areas; the transition from Philanthropies to Elevate represents a **new funding cycle** — ideal timing for a new relationship
3. **Accessibility Nonprofit Tech Accelerator** — AI-AA could be an NTA participant
4. **Microsoft for Nonprofits** — technology grants and discounts as a starting relationship

### Strategic Fit
- Microsoft already spends heavily on accessibility but lacks the **civil-rights policy infrastructure** that gives their tools institutional recognition in regulated settings
- The EY/Copilot study proves Microsoft's own products function as assistive technology — AI-AA provides the legal doctrine that formalizes this
- Brad Smith's five-year plan (2021) enters its final year — Elevate ($4B+) represents the **next chapter**. AI-AA's timing is optimal.
- Microsoft's existing disability-rights relationships (Valuable 500, AAPD, Be My Eyes) demonstrate willingness to fund and partner with advocacy organizations
- Jenny Lay-Flurrie's personal identity (deaf) and leadership role mean accessibility is not a PR function — it's structural

### The Core Pitch to Microsoft
"Microsoft already invested $25M in AI for Accessibility and $4B+ in Elevate. Immersive Reader has 35 million users. Copilot was rated as assistive technology by 91% of disabled employees. But when a hospital, school, or government agency blocks AI tools, your investment hits a wall. AI-AA builds the legal doctrine and institutional standards that remove that wall — so your accessibility products work where they matter most."

## 10. Risks and Objections

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "We already fund accessibility extensively" | Without legal recognition, the tools you fund are optional conveniences in regulated settings. When a hospital blocks Copilot, your $25M AI for Accessibility investment doesn't help the patient trying to understand their diagnosis. AI-AA removes institutional barriers so your tools work where stakes are highest. |
| "This sounds like lobbying" | AI-AA is a 501(c)(3). We produce doctrine, evidence, and model policies. We petition agencies for guidance — the same mechanism used for every prior auxiliary-aid recognition (screen readers, captioning, text-to-speech). |
| "We don't want to fund something that creates compliance obligations for us" | AI-AA's doctrine creates clarity, not liability. We argue that blocking AI comprehension tools *may* violate existing law. The framework says "yes" to AI tools under clear conditions. This expands your market — institutions that currently block AI tools would adopt them. |
| "It's too early / unproven" | Based on existing law (ADA effective-communication, auxiliary-aids). Not new law — new application. Your own Copilot study (91% call it assistive tech) is the evidence. Early sponsors shape the framework. |
| "Governance concerns — we don't want our name on positions we didn't approve" | AI-AA's governance firewall expressly denies funders control over legal positions or policy recommendations. Sponsors receive advisory seats only — no votes on mission or policy. This protects your reputation while letting you fund the mission. |
