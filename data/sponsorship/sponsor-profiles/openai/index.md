## 1. Company Overview

OpenAI was founded in 2015 as a nonprofit AI research lab. It has since converted into a for-profit capped-benefit entity and is in the process of restructuring into a Delaware Public Benefit Corporation (PBC). The company's mission has evolved through six iterations; as of the 2025 restructuring it reads: "OpenAI's mission is to ensure that artificial general intelligence benefits all of humanity." Notably, the word "safely" was removed from the mission statement during the for-profit conversion — a change that drew criticism from AI safety researchers and nonprofit accountability scholars.

**Headquarters:** San Francisco, CA
**CEO:** Sam Altman
**DC Office:** Opened July 2025, led by Chan Park (Head of Global Affairs, US & Canada) and Joe Larson (joining from Anduril). OpenAI spent $2.99M on federal lobbying in 2025, a near-sevenfold increase over prior years, working with DLA Piper and Akin Gump.

## 4. ChatGPT as a Comprehension Aid

ChatGPT already functions as a de facto comprehension aid for hundreds of millions of users. The following capabilities map directly to the auxiliary aid functions AI-AA's doctrine addresses.

### Advanced Voice Mode
- **What it does:** Real-time spoken conversation with ChatGPT, including camera-based visual analysis
- **Accessibility implications:** Blind/low-vision users can point their phone camera at text, objects, or environments and receive spoken descriptions and explanations. Users with reading disabilities can listen to explanations rather than read them.
- **Availability:** Free tier (10-15 min/day with GPT-4o Mini); Plus ($20/month, full access); Pro ($200/month, unlimited)
- **Key feature:** Video mode allows real-time environmental description — a direct parallel to Be My Eyes' human volunteer model

### Document Analysis & Summarization
- **Upload support:** PDFs, images, spreadsheets, code files
- **Core functions:**
  - Summarize lengthy documents into key points
  - Explain complex passages in simpler language
  - Define technical, legal, or medical terminology in context
  - Reformat dense text into structured, scannable layouts
  - Answer clarifying questions about meaning and implications
  - Translate into preferred reading level or communication style
- **Availability:** All Plus, Team, Enterprise, and Edu users

### ChatGPT Edu
Launched May 2024. Purpose-built for universities with enterprise-level security. Powered by GPT-4o.

- **Features:** Data analytics, web browsing, document summarization, custom GPTs, workspace sharing
- **Key deployment:** California State University system — 23 campuses, 500,000+ students. Largest single-organization ChatGPT deployment ever.
- **University partners:** Oxford, Wharton, UT Austin, Arizona State, Columbia, Harvard, MIT, Duke, Caltech, Texas A&M, Ohio State, Michigan
- **NextGenAI Consortium:** $50M in research grants, compute funding, and API access to support students, educators, and researchers
- **Accessibility use cases:** Personalized tutoring; language learning with tailored feedback; verbal feedback on assignments; accessibility for blind/low-vision students via audio interaction

### ChatGPT Enterprise
- **Seats:** 7 million+ across 1M+ organizations
- **Fortune 500 adoption:** 92%
- **Compliance:** Enterprise Compliance API for regulated industries (finance, healthcare, legal, government)
- **Security:** Data not used for training; SOC 2 Type II; customer-managed encryption keys

### ChatGPT Health (January 2026)
- Personal health literacy tool — connect medical records, Apple Health, MyFitnessPal, Function
- Understand test results, prepare for appointments, navigate health decisions
- **Not HIPAA-covered** — designed for personal use, not regulated healthcare
- No BAA offered for ChatGPT Health (distinct from ChatGPT for Healthcare enterprise product)

### ChatGPT for Healthcare (January 2026)
- Enterprise-grade AI for hospitals and clinicians
- **HIPAA-compliant:** BAA available; PHI not used for training
- **Deployments:** AdventHealth, Baylor Scott & White, Boston Children's Hospital, Cedars-Sinai, HCA Healthcare, Memorial Sloan Kettering, Stanford Medicine Children's Health, UCSF
- **Use cases:** Patient chart summarization, care coordination, discharge workflows, prior authorization
- **Partners:** Abridge, Ambience, EliseAI (ambient listening, clinical documentation, appointment scheduling)

### ChatGPT Gov (January 2025)
- Deployable on Azure Government cloud
- **Compliance:** IL5, CJIS, ITAR, FedRAMP High
- **Adoption:** 90,000+ users across 3,500+ federal, state, and local agencies; 18M+ messages
- **GSA partnership:** $1/agency/year for ChatGPT Enterprise access
- **DOD contract:** $200M ceiling through Chief Digital and AI Office (CDAO)
- **National Labs:** Los Alamos, Lawrence Livermore, Sandia
- **Agency partners:** Air Force Research Lab, NASA, NIH, Treasury

### Operator (January 2025)
- AI agent that navigates the web on behalf of users
- **Accessibility potential:** Could enable users with motor or visual disabilities to complete web tasks via voice commands without manual navigation
- **Status:** Available to Pro subscribers; still in research phase for accessibility applications

## 6. OpenAI's Disability & Accessibility Programs

### What Exists

OpenAI does **not** have a formal, named accessibility program comparable to Microsoft's AI for Accessibility ($25M) or Google's AI for Social Good. Their accessibility contributions are primarily through:

1. **Be My Eyes partnership** — The flagship accessibility collaboration
2. **Advanced Voice Mode** — De facto accessibility tool for blind/low-vision users
3. **ChatGPT Edu** — Accessibility features for students with disabilities
4. **Operator** — Potential motor/visual accessibility tool (early stage)
5. **People-First AI Fund** — Some grantees may have accessibility focus, but no explicit disability category
6. **Mentra investment** — Sam Altman's personal $1M investment in neurodivergent employment (via Hydrazine Capital, not OpenAI corporate)

### What Does NOT Exist

- No dedicated accessibility team or Chief Accessibility Officer
- No named accessibility grant program
- No published accessibility policy or commitment
- No formal disability inclusion statement beyond standard EEO
- No visible accessibility advocate in the leadership team
- No accessibility-focused category in the OpenAI Foundation's grant programs

### The Gap

This is the most important finding for AI-AA's pitch: **OpenAI is the world's largest AI company with 900 million weekly users, powers the most successful AI accessibility tool ever built (Be My AI), and yet has no formal accessibility program, no accessibility policy team, and no framework for how its tools serve as accessibility aids in regulated settings.** AI-AA fills this gap.
