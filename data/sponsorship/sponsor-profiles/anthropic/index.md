## 1. Company Overview

Anthropic was founded in 2021 by Dario Amodei (CEO) and Daniela Amodei (President), along with several former OpenAI researchers. The company's stated mission is "the responsible development and maintenance of advanced AI for the long-term benefit of humanity." Anthropic is headquartered in San Francisco with a new Washington, DC office opened in 2026.

Anthropic is the creator of Claude, a family of AI assistants known for their emphasis on safety, helpfulness, and honesty. The company pioneered Constitutional AI (CAI), a training methodology that aligns AI behavior with explicit principles rather than purely human feedback. Notably, research on Anthropic's public constitution showed lower bias scores across social dimensions — including Disability Status — compared to their standard model.

**AI-AA note:** Anthropic is AI-AA's most natural first sponsor. AI-AA is built on Claude, Anthropic's responsible AI positioning directly aligns with our mission, and Claude's deployments in healthcare, legal, and education are exactly the sectors where our doctrine applies.

## 3. Claude for Nonprofits Program

Anthropic launched **Claude for Nonprofits** on Giving Tuesday (December 2, 2025), establishing the company's formal nonprofit engagement infrastructure:

- **Pricing:** 70-75% discount on Claude business tools for qualified nonprofits

## 4. Claude as a Comprehension Aid

Claude is deployed across the exact sectors where AI-AA's doctrine applies — healthcare, education, legal, and government — making it a de facto comprehension aid for millions of users.

### Claude for Healthcare (January 2026)
Launched at the J.P. Morgan Healthcare Conference, Claude for Healthcare is purpose-built for providers and payers:
- **HIPAA-ready infrastructure** for enterprise healthcare customers
- **Medical record integration:** Users can connect personal health records to Claude for plain-language Q&A
- **Patient education:** Summarize medical history, explain test results in plain language, detect patterns across health metrics, prepare appointment questions
- **Clinical operations:** Prior authorization, care coordination, regulatory submissions
- **Medical databases:** Native integrations with CMS Coverage Database, ICD-10 codes, PubMed
- **Deployment:** Banner Health deployed Claude-based platform "BannerWise" to 55,000+ employees across six states (late 2025)
- **Microsoft Foundry partnership:** Claude available in Microsoft Foundry for healthcare and life sciences customers

**AI-AA relevance:** When hospitals deploy anti-AI measures on patient portals, Claude for Healthcare's patient education capabilities are exactly the comprehension aids being blocked. Our doctrine protects this use case.

### Claude for Life Sciences (October 2025)
- Models trained specifically for life sciences tasks
- Native integrations to medical and scientific databases
- Used for drug development, regulatory submissions, clinical trial analysis

### Claude in Education
- Used by students and educators for text comprehension, essay feedback, and learning support
- ChatGPT Edu equivalent not yet launched by Anthropic, but Claude is widely used in academic settings
- Constitutional AI's lower disability bias score means Claude is particularly suitable for users with cognitive and neurodevelopmental disabilities

### Claude in Legal
- Document summarization and plain-language explanation of legal texts
- Contract review and interpretation
- Used by legal aid organizations for access-to-justice work
- Claude for Nonprofits explicitly supports legal compliance documentation

### General Comprehension Aid Functions
Claude performs all the core comprehension functions our doctrine addresses:
- **Summarize** lengthy documents into key points
- **Explain** complex passages in simpler language
- **Define** technical, legal, or medical terminology in context
- **Reformat** dense text into structured, scannable layouts
- **Answer clarifying questions** about meaning and implications
- **Translate** into preferred reading level or communication style

## 5. Responsible AI Positioning

Anthropic's brand is built on responsible AI. This is not a CSR add-on — it is the company's core identity.

### Constitutional AI (CAI)
- Training methodology that uses explicit written principles to guide AI behavior
- Public constitution research showed **lower bias scores for Disability Status** and Physical Appearance compared to standard models
- The public constitution emphasizes accessibility — which may explain the greater reduction in disability-related bias

### Responsible Scaling Policy (RSP)
- Anthropic's framework for evaluating and managing risks as AI capabilities increase
- Commits to not deploying models that exceed specific capability thresholds without corresponding safety measures
- Published and regularly updated

### AI Safety Research
- Anthropic publishes extensively on AI safety, interpretability, and alignment
- The company frames safety not as a constraint on capability but as an enabler of trustworthy deployment
- This framing directly maps to AI-AA's argument: safe, responsible AI tools deserve institutional recognition as legitimate accessibility aids

### Key Alignment with AI-AA
"Responsible AI positioning without legal recognition is incomplete." — This is the core pitch to Anthropic. Their products are built to be trustworthy, but without the policy infrastructure AI-AA provides, institutions still block them. AI-AA's work makes Anthropic's responsible AI investment durable.
