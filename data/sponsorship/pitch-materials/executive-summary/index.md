# AI Access Alliance — Executive Summary

*For Prospective Founding Sponsors*

---

## The Problem

Millions of people with cognitive disabilities, neurodivergence, and reading-related disabilities now rely on AI tools to understand critical documents — medical forms, lease agreements, benefits notices, school paperwork, legal contracts. These tools summarize, explain, define terms, and reformat complex text into language the user can act on.

But the institutions where comprehension matters most — hospitals, schools, government agencies, courts, employers — have no clear rules for when a person's AI comprehension tool is a legitimate accessibility aid. Many actively block AI access. The result: the people who need comprehension support the most lose it in the settings where the stakes are highest.

**This is an accessibility crisis hiding in plain sight.**

## The Legal Foundation

This is not a new-law problem. It is a recognition problem.

The ADA and Section 504 already require covered entities to provide **auxiliary aids and services** for effective communication (28 CFR 36.303). The law explicitly contemplates evolving technology — screen readers, captioning, and text-to-speech were all once novel. AI comprehension tools are the next category of auxiliary aid, performing the same function for cognitive and reading-related disabilities that screen readers perform for blindness.

When an institution blocks a person's authorized AI comprehension tool from accessing text the person is already entitled to read, it may be denying an auxiliary aid under existing law.

## The Solution

**AI Access Alliance** is a 501(c)(3) non-profit that builds the legal, policy, and institutional infrastructure needed for AI comprehension tools to be recognized as legitimate accessibility accommodations.

We produce four things:

| Workstream | Output |
|------------|--------|
| **Legal & Policy** | Doctrine memo, guidance petitions to DOJ/HHS/ED, model ADA/504 framing |
| **Evidence & Research** | User stories, sector case studies, expert advisory council, harm documentation |
| **Corporate Standards** | Model "allowable AI comprehension" policies, technical design principles, trust & safety guardrails |
| **Coalition & Public Education** | Disability-rights coalition, media narrative, cross-sector coalition statement |

## Governance & Independence

AI-AA is a disability-rights organization, not an industry association. Our governance ensures mission integrity:

- Board and advisory council are **disability-led**
- Sponsor agreements **expressly deny** funders control over legal positions, enforcement targets, or policy recommendations
- Sponsors receive advisory seats only — **no votes** on legal or policy decisions
- All policy positions are developed independently and published without sponsor review

This firewall protects both AI-AA's credibility and our sponsors' reputations.
