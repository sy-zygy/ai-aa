# Pitch Materials

**Owner:** Sponsorships Director | **Status:** In Development | **Updated:** 2026-04-06

All sponsor-facing documents. Each piece is designed for a specific moment in the outreach sequence — from first contact to close.

## Materials Inventory

| Document | Purpose | Stage | Status |
|----------|---------|-------|--------|
| [[Executive Summary]] | 1-page written elevator pitch | Initial outreach / follow-up | **Complete** |
| [[Sponsor Deck]] | 10-15 slide visual presentation | First meeting / deep dive | **Complete** |
| Full Proposal (per-sponsor) | Customized proposal with budget, milestones, governance | Deep dive / negotiation | Not started |
| Sponsor Agreement Template | Legal template with governance firewall | Negotiation / close | Not started |
| Impact Report Template | Quarterly progress report for sponsors | Post-close / stewardship | Not started |

## Usage Guide

- **Cold outreach:** Executive Summary only (attached to intro email)
- **First meeting:** Sponsor Deck (presented live, not sent ahead)
- **Follow-up package:** Executive Summary + 2-3 relevant user stories + advisory council list
- **Deep dive:** Full Proposal (customized per sponsor) + Sponsor Deck
- **Close:** Sponsor Agreement Template
- **Ongoing:** Quarterly Impact Reports

## Customization Notes

Every material must be tailored per sponsor. At minimum:
- Reference their specific accessibility/AI programs by name
- Include alignment hooks specific to their products and strategy
- Cite user stories featuring their products as comprehension aids (when available)
- Match the ask to their existing budget lines

Generic materials signal that we haven't done our homework. Customize everything.
