## What's Missing (Your Sprint 1 Deliverables)

These are ordered by priority. Items 1-3 are the critical path — without them, no outreach can begin.

### 1. Tier 1 Sponsor Profiles (Priority: Critical)

Build deep research profiles for all 5 founding sponsor targets. Each profile must follow the template in [[Sponsor Profiles]] and include:

- Company overview and relevant business context
- Specific accessibility/AI programs with dollar amounts and program leads
- Alignment analysis — why AI-AA maps to their existing strategy
- Their products that function as comprehension aids (specific product names, use cases)
- Entry points — specific teams, people, and programs to approach
- Budget alignment — which existing budget line funds us
- Customized value proposition
- Anticipated objections and pre-drafted responses
- Pipeline status and next action

**Targets (in priority order):**
1. **Microsoft** — AI for Accessibility ($25M), Immersive Reader, Seeing AI, Copilot
2. **Google** — AI Opportunity Fund ($75M), Google.org, Google for Education
3. **Anthropic** — Responsible AI positioning, Claude in healthcare/legal/education
4. **OpenAI** — ChatGPT as de facto comprehension tool, nonprofits program
5. **Meta** — AI Glasses Impact Grants, Ray-Ban Meta accessibility features

### 2. Corporate Sponsorship Acquisition Playbook (Priority: Critical)

The hub page references [[Corporate Sponsorship Acquisition]] but the page doesn't exist. Create it at `/sponsorship/corporate-sponsorship-acquisition/index.md`. This is your operational playbook — the outreach sequence, per-sponsor strategy, objection handling, timeline, and pipeline management methodology. Your persona already has strong content on playbook and objection handling — formalize it as the canonical KB page.

### 3. Sponsor Deck (Priority: Critical)

The pitch materials inventory lists the Sponsor Deck as "Complete" but it doesn't exist. Create a 10-15 slide deck outline at `/sponsorship/pitch-materials/sponsor-deck/index.md`. This is the primary meeting tool — the visual presentation for first meetings and deep dives. Structure:

1. The problem (comprehension barriers in high-stakes settings)
2. A human story (concrete, emotional, specific)
3. The legal foundation (ADA/504 auxiliary aids — existing law, not new law)
4. What AI-AA does (4 workstreams, tangible outputs)
5. Year-one milestones
6. Who's involved (advisory council, coalition partners)
7. Sponsor tiers and benefits
8. Governance & independence (the firewall)
9. The ask
10. Next steps

### 4. Warm Introduction Mapping (Priority: High)

For each Tier 1 target, identify the most promising path to a warm introduction. Map out:
- Conference overlap opportunities (upcoming accessibility/AI conferences)
- Advisory council members who have connections to targets
- Industry working groups where AI-AA and targets might intersect
- Any other creative entry points

### 5. Grant Opportunities Page (Priority: Medium)

Create `/sponsorship/grant-opportunities/index.md` — initial research on foundation and government grants relevant to AI-AA's mission. This is a secondary funding track but should be on the radar.

## Cross-Department Requests

As you build sponsor profiles and pitch materials, you'll need inputs from other departments:

- **Research Director:** Request user stories and harm examples that feature each sponsor's products. A story about someone using Copilot to understand a medical form, or ChatGPT to parse a lease agreement, is pitch gold.
- **Policy Director:** Get the latest on the doctrine memo timeline. Sponsors need to see that the legal argument is rigorous and progressing.
- **Communications Director:** Align on public narrative and messaging. Sponsor-facing materials must be consistent with public-facing materials.
- **Operations Director:** Confirm governance firewall language for sponsor agreements. This is non-negotiable in every pitch.

## Timeline

Sprint 1 target: complete within 2 weeks. The goal is to have outreach-ready materials by mid-April so we can begin real sponsor conversations in Q2 2026. Corporate budget cycles are slow — every week of delay pushes potential commitments further out.

## Governance Reminder

In every piece of content you create, reinforce the firewall: sponsors fund the mission, they don't direct it. This is not a disclaimer to add at the end — it's a structural principle that shapes how we pitch, negotiate, and report.
