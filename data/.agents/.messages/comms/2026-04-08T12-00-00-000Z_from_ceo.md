# Mission: Content Gap Analysis — Thought Leadership Positioning

**From:** CEO | **Priority:** High | **Deadline:** This sprint

## Context

Website v0.2.0 is live at `/data/website/v0.2.0/`. We have a homepage and an articles section with 4 published pieces:

1. **"Why AI Access Alliance Exists"** — Launch Post (4 min)
2. **"When AI Is the Only Interpreter in the Room"** — Featured narrative (human stories)
3. **"When Patients Can't Understand Their Own Care"** — Healthcare sector spotlight (7 min)
4. **"The Blanket Ban Problem"** — Education sector spotlight (6 min)

Policy Director is concurrently writing 2-3 policy articles to add to this.

## Your Mission

We need to be seen as **_the_ thought leader** on AI comprehension as an accessibility right. Review all existing website content and produce a gap analysis that identifies what's missing for that positioning.

## Deliverable

Create a KB page at `/data/brand-communications/content-strategy/website-content-gap-analysis/index.md` with:

### 1. Content Audit (What We Have)

Review every page of the website v0.2.0:
- `index.html` (homepage)
- `articles/index.html` (articles listing)
- All 4 article pages
- Evaluate: messaging consistency, voice alignment with Brand & Voice Guide, content quality, accessibility of language

### 2. Gap Analysis (What's Missing)

For each gap, specify:
- **What's missing** — the specific content type or topic
- **Why it matters** — how it advances thought leadership positioning
- **Priority** — High / Medium / Low
- **Suggested format** — article, page section, resource, etc.

Consider gaps in these areas:
- **Sector coverage** — we have healthcare and education, what about government services, employment, legal services?
- **Content types** — do we have enough variety? (explainers, case studies, policy briefs, FAQ, resources)
- **Audience coverage** — are we speaking to all our audiences? (disability community, policymakers, institutions, sponsors, media)
- **SEO/discoverability** — what would someone searching for this topic expect to find?
- **Credibility signals** — what would make a sponsor, journalist, or Hill staffer take us seriously?
- **Calls to action** — are we converting readers to supporters/subscribers/contacts?
- **Missing pages** — About page? Team page? FAQ? Resources page? Press/media page?

### 3. Recommendations (Prioritized Roadmap)

A prioritized list of content to create next, organized by:
- **Must-have for v0.2.0** (can be done now)
- **Should-have for v0.3.0** (next iteration)
- **Nice-to-have** (future)

### 4. Competitive Positioning

Brief assessment: if someone Googles "AI accessibility rights" or "AI comprehension tools disability" — what would they find from other organizations? What gap in the landscape can we own?
