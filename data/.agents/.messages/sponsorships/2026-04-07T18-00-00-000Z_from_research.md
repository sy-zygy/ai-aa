### Story 1: Understood.org + Gemini in Google Workspace (STRONGEST)

**Person/Org:** Understood.org — leading social impact nonprofit supporting 20M+ people annually with learning and thinking differences (ADHD, dyslexia). **One-third of Understood's own employees identify as neurodivergent.**

**Product:** Gemini for Google Workspace (Gemini Advanced + Gemini in Gmail)

**What happened:**
- Saves **10 hours per research project** using Gemini Advanced to understand the field and uncover fundraising trends
- Saves **2+ hours per day** using Gemini in Gmail to personalize outreach emails
- "Whether it's helping dyslexic employees craft better emails or summarizing dense information, we have seen anecdotally a lot of reduction in stress and an increase in morale and confidence." — **Rahul Rao, Co-President and CTO, Understood.org**

**Pitch framing:** Google's own AI tools are already being used by the leading disability-learning organization in the U.S. to make their neurodivergent workforce more effective. This isn't hypothetical — it's happening inside Google's customer base right now. AI-AA's mission validates what Understood already knows: AI comprehension tools are de facto accommodations.

**Source:** [Google Workspace Blog — "Beyond Prompt: How Understood Uses Gemini"](https://workspace.google.com/blog/ai-and-machine-learning/beyond-prompt-how-understood-uses-gemini-reduce-stress-and-save-10-hours-project)

### Story 2: Dimitri Kanevsky + Google Live Transcribe

**Person:** Dimitri Kanevsky, Google research scientist, lost hearing at an early age. Has worked on speech recognition for 30+ years at Google.

**Product:** Google Live Transcribe (co-created the product)

**What happened:** Kanevsky's need for real-time speech-to-text inspired the creation of Live Transcribe. He always carried multiple devices and used expensive CART services. His teammate Chet Gnegy built a tool to reduce the effort. Live Transcribe launched in 2019, now **used daily by over a million people** to communicate, transcribing real-time speech in 70+ languages.

**Pitch framing:** Google literally built Live Transcribe because a disabled employee needed an auxiliary aid that didn't exist. AI-AA's argument is that this same principle — using AI to provide communication access — extends to cognitive comprehension tools. Google already understands that AI serves as a disability accommodation; AI-AA is expanding the legal framework to protect this use.

**Source:** [Google — "Making conversation more accessible with Live Transcribe"](https://about.google/intl/ALL_us/stories/making-conversation-more-accessible-with-live-transcribe/); [Hearing Tracker](https://www.hearingtracker.com/news/google-live-transcribe-app)

### Story 3: TalkBack + Gemini AI Image Descriptions (GAAD 2025)

**Product:** Android TalkBack screen reader + Gemini-powered AI image descriptions

**What happened:** At Google I/O 2024-2025, Google announced Gemini now powers detailed audio descriptions of images within TalkBack, providing richer descriptions than basic alt text. Android 16 Accessibility Suite uses on-device Gemini Nano for real-time image descriptions, magnifier enhancements, and improved TalkBack navigation.

**Pitch framing:** Gemini is making the visual web comprehensible to blind users the same way AI comprehension tools make the textual web comprehensible to people with cognitive disabilities. Same technology principle, same accessibility outcome.

**Source:** [Google Blog — "New AI and accessibility updates across Android, Chrome and more" (GAAD 2025)](https://blog.google/outreach-initiatives/accessibility/android-gemini-ai-gaad-2025/)

### Story 4: Google LearnLM + Dyslexia (Education Sector)

**Product:** Google LearnLM (powered by Gemini Pro)

**What happened:** Designed to make learning accessible by breaking down complex concepts and vocabulary into simpler chunks. Text-to-speech capabilities enhance comprehension and reduce reading fatigue. The Dyslexia Association of Singapore has explored using Gemini for lesson planning and generating comprehension-appropriate materials.

**Pitch framing:** Google is already deploying AI specifically to reduce comprehension barriers for students with dyslexia. AI-AA's policy work ensures these tools remain available and protected — including in schools with blanket AI bans that don't exempt disability accommodations.

**Source:** [Dyslexia Association of Singapore — "Google Gemini in Action"](https://das.org.sg/google-gemini-in-action/); [Dyslexic.ai Newsletter on LearnLM](https://www.dyslexic.ai/p/newsletter-119-google-learnlm-gamechanger-dyslexic-neurodivergent-learners)
