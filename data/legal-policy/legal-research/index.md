# Legal Research

**Owner:** Policy Director | **Status:** Active | **Updated:** 2026-04-05

This section compiles the statutory authorities, regulations, agency guidance, case law, and secondary sources that underpin AI-AA's legal arguments. All citations in the [[Core Doctrine Memo]] trace back to sources documented here.

## Primary Statutory Authorities

### Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA)

| Provision | Citation | Key Language |
|-----------|----------|-------------|
| Auxiliary aids requirement (Title III) | 42 U.S.C. § 12182(b)(2)(A)(iii) | Discrimination includes "failure to take such steps as may be necessary to ensure that no individual with a disability is excluded... because of the absence of auxiliary aids and services" |
| Definition of auxiliary aids | 42 U.S.C. § 12103(1) | Non-exhaustive list including "other effective methods" and "other similar services and actions" |
| Definition of disability | 42 U.S.C. § 12102(1)(A) | "Physical or mental impairment that substantially limits one or more major life activities" |
| Major life activities | 42 U.S.C. § 12102(2)(A) | Explicitly includes "reading" and "communicating" |
| Title II (public entities) | 42 U.S.C. § 12131-12165 | State and local government nondiscrimination |

### ADA Amendments Act of 2008

| Provision | Citation | Key Language |
|-----------|----------|-------------|
| Broadened disability definition | Pub. L. 110-325, § 2(b)(5) | "The question of whether an individual's impairment is a disability under the ADA should not demand extensive analysis" |
| Rules of construction | 42 U.S.C. § 12102(4)(A) | Disability shall be "construed in favor of broad coverage" |

### Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act

| Provision | Citation | Key Language |
|-----------|----------|-------------|
| Nondiscrimination | 29 U.S.C. § 794 | "No otherwise qualified individual with a disability... shall, solely by reason of her or his disability, be excluded from the participation in, be denied the benefits of, or be subjected to discrimination under any program or activity receiving Federal financial assistance" |

### Section 1557 of the Affordable Care Act

| Provision | Citation | Key Language |
|-----------|----------|-------------|
| Nondiscrimination in health programs | 42 U.S.C. § 18116 | Incorporates Section 504 nondiscrimination protections for health programs receiving federal financial assistance |

### IDEA (Individuals with Disabilities Education Act)

| Provision | Citation | Key Language |
|-----------|----------|-------------|
| Assistive technology device | 20 U.S.C. § 1401(1) | "Any item, piece of equipment, or product system... that is used to increase, maintain, or improve functional capabilities of a child with a disability" |
| Assistive technology service | 20 U.S.C. § 1401(2) | "Any service that directly assists a child with a disability in the selection, acquisition, or use of an assistive technology device" |

## Agency Guidance

### DOJ

- [Effective Communication (ADA.gov)](https://www.ada.gov/resources/effective-communication/) — Confirms auxiliary aids list is non-exhaustive; "new devices are being invented and new technologies developed"
- [ADA Technical Assistance Letters](https://www.justice.gov/crt/americans-disabilities-act-technical-assistance-letters-33) — Historical letters interpreting ADA provisions
- [2024 Title II Web Accessibility Rule](https://www.ada.gov/law-and-regs/regulations/title-ii-web-app-accessibility/) — Establishes WCAG 2.1 AA as baseline for state/local government web content

### HHS

- [HRSA Effective Communication Technical Assistance](https://www.hrsa.gov/sites/default/files/hrsa/about/organization/bureaus/ocrdi/effective-communication-ta.pdf) — Guidance on effective communication in healthcare settings
- Section 1557 Final Rule (2024) — Updated nondiscrimination requirements for health programs
