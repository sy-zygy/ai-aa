# Guidance Petitions

**Owner:** Policy Director | **Status:** Planning | **Updated:** 2026-04-05

This section will contain formal requests to federal agencies asking for guidance on whether AI comprehension tools qualify as auxiliary aids under existing law.

## Planned Petitions

| # | Agency | Legal Basis | Focus | Target | Status |
|---|--------|------------|-------|--------|--------|
| 1 | DOJ Civil Rights Division | ADA Titles II & III | General effective-communication guidance for AI comprehension tools | Q4 2026 | Not started |
| 2 | HHS Office for Civil Rights | Section 1557, Section 504 | Healthcare-specific guidance; patient portal compatibility | Q4 2026 | Not started |
| 3 | Dept of Education OCR | Section 504, IDEA | Education-specific guidance; AI policy exemptions for accessibility | Q4 2026 | Not started |

## Sequencing

The guidance petitions depend on the [[Core Doctrine Memo]] — the legal argument must be fully developed before we make formal requests of agencies. The doctrine memo provides the analytical foundation; the petitions operationalize it into specific asks.

**Pre-requisites before drafting petitions:**
- [ ] Core Doctrine Memo finalized (v1.0)
- [ ] Evidence base from Research sufficient to support sector-specific claims
- [ ] Advisory council review of doctrine memo
- [ ] At least 2-3 documented user stories per target sector

## Petition Format (Planned)

Each petition will follow a standard structure:

1. **Introduction** — AI-AA identification, mission, standing
2. **Legal authority** — Statutory and regulatory basis for the request
3. **Factual basis** — Evidence of harm, user stories, sector data
4. **The ask** — Specific guidance requested (FAQ, guidance letter, or technical assistance)
5. **Proposed framework** — How the agency could address the issue within existing regulatory tools
6. **Supporting materials** — Doctrine memo, evidence appendix, user story summaries
