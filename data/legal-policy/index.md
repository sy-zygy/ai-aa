# Legal & Policy

**Owner:** Policy Director | **Status:** Active | **Updated:** 2026-04-06

The Legal & Policy department is the intellectual engine of AI Access Alliance. Everything AI-AA does — sponsor pitches, public messaging, coalition building, guidance petitions — rests on the legal and policy foundation this department produces.

## Department Scope

This department owns two of AI-AA's four charter workstreams:

1.  **Legal & Policy Workstream** — The doctrinal foundation: white papers, guidance petitions, model framing memos that establish AI comprehension tools as auxiliary aids under existing law.
    
2.  **Corporate Standards Workstream** — The implementation layer: model policies, technical design principles, and trust-and-safety guardrails that covered entities can adopt.
    

### What's In Scope

-   Legal analysis of ADA, Section 504, Section 1557, and effective-communication requirements
    
-   Drafting doctrine memos, guidance petition requests, and model policies
    
-   Defining technical standards for AI comprehension tools that qualify as auxiliary aids
    
-   Trust-and-safety frameworks that distinguish legitimate comprehension aids from unauthorized scraping or autonomous action
    
-   Monitoring and responding to relevant regulatory developments, enforcement actions, and case law
    

### What's Out of Scope (Year One)

-   Generalized scraping rights or interoperability mandates
    
-   Autonomous agent access to systems
    
-   Universal access claims for all AI tools
    
-   Litigation strategy or legal defense (future phase)
    
-   International disability law (future phase)
    

This boundary is critical. If the legal argument bleeds into general "AI rights" territory, the entire advocacy position becomes vulnerable to "industry front" attacks.

## Core Argument

The legal argument rests on four established principles:

1.  **Effective communication is already required.** ADA and Section 504 require covered entities to provide auxiliary aids and services. (28 CFR 36.303)
    
2.  **Auxiliary aids evolve with technology.** The law explicitly contemplates new technologies serving as auxiliary aids. Screen readers, captioning, and text-to-speech were all once novel.
    
3.  **AI comprehension tools are the next auxiliary aid.** For people with cognitive disabilities, neurodivergence, or reading-related disabilities, AI tools that summarize, explain, define, and reformat text serve the same function as screen readers do for blind users.
    
4.  **Blocking these tools can constitute an accessibility barrier.** When an institution prevents a user's authorized AI tool from accessing text the user is already entitled to read, it may be denying an auxiliary aid.

## Operating Model

### How This Department Works

-   **Precision over speed.** Every claim must be defensible under scrutiny from lawyers, policymakers, and critics.
    
-   **Plain-language summaries for everything.** Every technical legal document gets an accessible summary. If the people AI-AA advocates for can't understand our work, we've failed.
    
-   **Primary sources first.** ADA text, CFR sections, DOJ guidance letters, case law, agency technical assistance documents.
    
-   **Anticipate objections.** Every argument includes the strongest counterargument and our response.
    
-   **Feed other teams.** Doctrine shapes messaging (Comms), anchors pitches (Development), and informs governance (Operations).
