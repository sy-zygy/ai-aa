# Model Policies

**Owner:** Policy Director | **Status:** Planning | **Updated:** 2026-04-05

This section will contain template policies that covered entities can adopt to address AI comprehension tools within their accessibility frameworks.

## Planned Deliverables

| # | Deliverable | Description | Target | Status |
|---|-------------|-------------|--------|--------|
| 1 | Model "Allowable AI Comprehension Support" Policy | Template that hospitals, universities, government agencies, and employers can adopt to define what AI comprehension tools are permitted, what guardrails apply, and how accommodation requests are handled | Q4 2026 | Not started |
| 2 | ADA/504 Framing Memo | Template that organizations can use to frame their AI accessibility policies in terms of existing legal obligations | Q3 2026 | Not started |
| 3 | Technical Design Principles | Standards for AI comprehension tools that qualify as accessibility aids: user authorization, data minimization, reliability, audit trails | Q3 2026 | Not started |

## Approach

These model policies translate the legal framework established in the [[Core Doctrine Memo]] into practical institutional guidance. They should be:

- **Adoptable** — Written so that a hospital, university, or government agency can adopt them with minimal customization
- **Legally grounded** — Each provision traces back to specific ADA/504/1557 requirements
- **Sector-specific** — Healthcare, education, and government versions with relevant regulatory context
- **Balanced** — Include legitimate safeguards (data security, reliability standards) alongside accommodation requirements

## Dependencies

- [[Core Doctrine Memo]] must be finalized before model policies can be drafted (legal foundation)
- [[Trust & Safety Guardrails]] framework should inform the guardrail provisions in model policies
- Sector case studies from Research will inform sector-specific customizations
