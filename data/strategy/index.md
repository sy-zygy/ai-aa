# Strategy & Roadmap

**Owner:** CEO | **Status:** Active | **Updated:** 2026-04-07

This is AI Access Alliance's strategic hub. It defines where we're going, how we get there, and how we measure progress. Everything in this section flows from the [[AI Access Alliance Overview]] charter.

## Strategic Position

AI-AA exists to fill a specific gap: there is no organization building the legal, policy, and public infrastructure needed for AI comprehension tools to be recognized as legitimate accessibility accommodations. The law already supports this — ADA effective-communication requirements and auxiliary-aids principles cover evolving technology. But no one has assembled the doctrine, evidence, coalition backing, and institutional standards to make it real.

That's what we do.

## Year-One Objective

**Win formal recognition that blocking a person's user-authorized AI comprehension tool can be an accessibility barrier under existing effective-communication and auxiliary-aids principles (ADA, Section 504, Section 1557).**

This means: by end of year one, at least one federal agency has issued guidance, published an FAQ, or accepted a petition that treats AI comprehension tools as a category of auxiliary aid worth considering under existing law.

## Strategic Priorities

Applied in this order when deciding what to work on:

1. **Does it advance the MVP mission?** If not, defer it.
2. **Does it unlock sponsorship?** Sponsors fund everything else. Bias toward sponsor-visible outputs.
3. **Does it build coalition legitimacy?** Disability-rights backing is the firewall against "industry front" criticism.
4. **Does it produce a tangible deliverable?** Ideas without artifacts don't move the needle.

## The Growth Flywheel

This is the engine that drives AI-AA from zero to a functioning advocacy organization:

```
Human Stories --> Public Attention --> Cause Validation --> Sponsors --> Research & Policy --> More Stories
```

Every team output should advance at least one stage of this flywheel.

## Cross-Department Dependencies

No department works in isolation. These are the critical handoffs:

- **Research --> Policy:** Evidence citations for doctrine memo and guidance petitions
- **Research --> Communications:** Human stories for social media and public narrative
- **Research --> Development:** Proof points and impact data for sponsor pitches
- **Policy --> Communications:** Legal framing shapes public messaging
- **Policy --> Development:** Doctrine memo is the anchor of the sponsor pitch
- **Operations --> Development:** Sponsor agreements must include governance firewall provisions
- **Communications --> Development:** Public traction validates the cause for sponsors
- **Operations --> All:** Governance structure is the credibility insurance for the entire organization

## Multi-Year Vision

**Year One (2026):** Establish the issue. Doctrine, evidence, coalition, sponsors, first guidance request.

**Year Two (2027):** Win the first formal recognition. Agency guidance, pilot programs in healthcare or education, expanded coalition. Begin sector-specific model policies.

**Year Three (2028):** Scale the impact. Multiple sector standards adopted, enforcement precedent established, international expansion explored. Evaluate 501(c)(4) affiliate for direct lobbying.

**Long-term:** Establish the norm that people should not lose meaningful access to digital information, services, or participation because they rely on AI systems to understand, navigate, or mediate information. Expand beyond comprehension to cover broader AI-mediated access.
