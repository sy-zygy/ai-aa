## Decision 1: Incorporate AI Access Alliance

**Status:** Articles drafted (Delaware template). Ready to file once state and founder details are confirmed.

### What This Does
Creates AI Access Alliance as a legal nonprofit corporation. This is the prerequisite for everything else — EIN, bank account, fiscal sponsorship, 501(c)(3) application, and receiving funds.

### State of Incorporation — Open Decision

The original plan was Delaware. After review, the best choice depends on where the founder lives:

| Factor | Delaware | Wyoming | DC | Founder's Home State |
|--------|---------|---------|-----|---------------------|
| Formation fee | $89 | $25 | $80 | ~$25-100 |
| Annual fees | $0 | $0 | $40/yr | Varies |
| Commercial registered agent needed? | Yes ($125/yr) | Yes (~$100/yr) | Yes (~$150/yr) | No — founder can serve |
| Foreign qualify in home state? | Yes (+$100-300) | Yes (+$100-300) | Yes (+$100-300) | No |
| Sponsor credibility | Strong | Slightly weak | Strong | Neutral |
| Policy/advocacy signal | Neutral | Neutral | **Strong** | Neutral |
| **Estimated Year 1 total** | **~$240+** | **~$125+** | **~$310+** | **~$75** (avg) |

**Note:** All out-of-state options require foreign qualification in the founder's home state, adding $100-$300+ in Year 1 and $50-$150/year ongoing. Incorporating in the home state avoids this entirely.

**Recommendations:**
- **Most states:** Incorporate in founder's home state (simplest, cheapest, no dual compliance)
- **If founder is in CA or NY:** Consider Delaware or DC (those states have burdensome nonprofit compliance)
- **If strong DC policy signal desired:** DC is the best fit for a federal policy/advocacy org
- **Wyoming:** Cheapest, but slight credibility risk with Fortune 500 sponsors — lean away

**Waiting on:** Founder's home state to finalize this decision.

## Decision 3: Fiscal Sponsorship (Bridge Funding)

**Status:** Research complete. DREDF recommended as primary target (Model C — preserves AI-AA's governance independence).

### What This Does
A fiscal sponsor is an existing 501(c)(3) that receives tax-deductible donations on our behalf while our own application is pending. This is how we accept sponsor money starting ~May, rather than waiting until Q3-Q4 for our own determination letter.

### Cost Breakdown

| Item | Cost | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| Fiscal sponsorship fee | **5-10% of funds received** | DREDF may reduce/waive for mission alignment |
| Upfront cost to us | **$0** | Fees are deducted from incoming funds, not paid out of pocket |

**On $500K Year 1 funding at 7%: ~$35K in fees.** This sounds significant, but:
- It comes out of sponsor money, not founder funds
- Without it, we simply cannot receive tax-deductible donations until the IRS determination letter arrives (Q3-Q4)
- The alternative is telling sponsors "we can't accept your money yet" — which kills deal momentum

### Can This Wait?

**The outreach should start now (costs $0), but the agreement won't execute until we have Articles filed.** The sequence is:
1. File Articles (Decision 1) -- *costs $89-$109*
2. Approach DREDF about fiscal sponsorship -- *costs $0*
3. Execute agreement after Articles are filed -- *costs $0 upfront*
4. Begin receiving sponsor funds -- *fees come from incoming funds*

### What I Need From You

1. **Do you have any connection to DREDF?** A warm intro would accelerate the conversation. If not, Operations will reach out cold — that's fine, just slower.
2. **Approval to proceed with cold outreach if no warm intro.** (Already approved in prior session — confirming this still stands.)
